\section{General Relativity as Geometric Compression}

Rather than encoding probability amplitudes across abstract configuration spaces, General Relativity encodes the causal and relational structure of spacetime itself.

In GR, physical force is replaced by smooth manifold curvature, and localized acceleration under interaction is replaced by inertial motion along geometric geodesics.

The complete geometric content of a gravitational field is captured by the four-index Riemann curvature tensor $R_{abcd}$.
Through its standard algebraic decomposition, it splits into the Ricci tensor (and scalar), which couple directly to local matter, and the trace-free \textbf{Weyl tensor} $C_{abcd}$:

\[
R_{abcd} = C_{abcd} + \text{(Ricci Terms)}
\]

The Weyl tensor represents the free, propagating degrees of freedom of the gravitational field—the pure, intrinsic structure of the spacetime vacuum independent of local mass-energy density.
Crucially, the Weyl tensor admits native eigenstructure decompositions (such as the Petrov classification), strongly implying a hidden spectral interpretation embedded directly within smooth classical geometry.

General Relativity, at its core, appears as a highly advanced geometric compression scheme designed to encode the relational, causal matrix of the cosmos.

In both systems, absolute, privileged reference frames are utterly discarded and replaced by pure relational networks.

To conceptualize geometric compression, let us recall our hourglass metaphor.

Now, let us scale this logic to its absolute limit: given a $3+1$ dimensional spacetime manifold, what is the highest possible compression efficiency a physical system could ever theoretically achieve?

The answer is a black hole.

The physics of black hole entropy represents the ultimate geometric data compression routine.

In contrast, boundaries can be defined with extreme mathematical economy within a low-dimensional geometric space. 

Geometry grants us the gift of \textbf{locality}.

Spacetime is not a fundamental material entity; it is a highly optimized geometric compression system. Informational boundaries are computationally cheap to describe and maintain as geometric descriptions.
