\chapter{Geometric Complexity: Full General Relativity}

\section{Minimum Description Length Functional}

We interpret spacetime geometry as a compression model for worldline and field data.
The optimal geometry minimizes total description length:

\begin{equation}
C_{\mathrm{total}}[g;\Phi]
=
C_G[g]
+
C_Q[\Phi \mid g],
\end{equation}

where:

\begin{align}
C_G[g]
&=
\alpha \int R \, \sqrt{-g} \, d^4x, \\
C_Q[\Phi \mid g]
&=
\beta \int \mathcal{L}_\text{matter}[\Phi, g] \, \sqrt{-g} \, d^4x.
\end{align}

Here:

\begin{itemize}
\item $C_G[g]$ is the geometric encoding cost (spacetime curvature), given by the Einstein–Hilbert action,
\item $C_Q[\Phi \mid g]$ is the conditional encoding cost of matter or fields $\Phi$ given the geometry $g$,
\item $R$ is the Ricci scalar curvature of the metric $g$,
\item $\sqrt{-g}\,d^4x$ is the invariant spacetime volume element.
\end{itemize}



\section{Variation with Respect to Matter / Worldlines}

Minimizing $C_Q[\Phi \mid g]$ with respect to matter degrees of freedom $\Phi$ yields the classical equations of motion for the fields:

\begin{equation}
\delta_\Phi C_Q = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad
\frac{\delta (\mathcal{L}_\text{matter} \sqrt{-g})}{\delta \Phi} = 0.
\end{equation}

For point particles, this reproduces the geodesic equation. For continuous fields (scalar, electromagnetic, fluid), it yields the usual Euler-Lagrange equations in curved spacetime.
Conceptually, these are the minimal conditional encoding trajectories or configurations for matter given the geometry.



\section{Variation with Respect to Geometry}

Varying the total functional with respect to the metric $g_{\mu\nu}$ gives:

\begin{equation}
\delta C_{\mathrm{total}} = \delta C_G + \delta C_Q = 0.
\end{equation}

The geometric term variation yields:

\begin{equation}
\delta C_G = \alpha \int G_{\mu\nu} \, \delta g^{\mu\nu} \, \sqrt{-g} \, d^4x,
\end{equation}

where $G_{\mu\nu} = R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2} g_{\mu\nu} R$ is the Einstein tensor.  

The matter term variation yields the stress-energy tensor:

\begin{equation}
\delta C_Q = -\frac{\beta}{2} \int T_{\mu\nu} \, \delta g^{\mu\nu} \, \sqrt{-g} \, d^4x, \quad
T_{\mu\nu} = -\frac{2}{\sqrt{-g}} \frac{\delta (\mathcal{L}_\text{matter} \sqrt{-g})}{\delta g^{\mu\nu}}.
\end{equation}

Setting \(\delta C_\text{total} = 0\) yields the full Einstein field equations:

\begin{equation}
G_{\mu\nu} = \kappa T_{\mu\nu}, \quad
\kappa = \frac{\beta}{\alpha}.
\end{equation}


\section{Conclusions}

In this framework:

\begin{itemize}
\item $C_G[g]$ represents the description length of the geometry itself.
\item $C_Q[\Phi \mid g]$ represents the description length of matter or field configurations given the geometry.
\item Minimizing $C_\text{total}$ finds the optimal compression of spacetime and matter histories.
\end{itemize}

This is a direct generalization of the spectral / wavefunction MDL argument from quantum mechanics to the full gravitational case.
Just as low spectral complexity histories dominate the wavefunction measure, low-geometric-complexity spacetime configurations dominate the MDL measure.

The principle naturally explains geodesic motion, energy-momentum conservation, and the Einstein field equations as outcomes of information-theoretic optimality.

Nonlinear GR is the optimal geometric compression of histories in spacetime.
