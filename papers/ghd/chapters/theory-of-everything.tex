\section{Everything is Information}

If we accept the foundational assumptions—universes are informational, observers are abstract patterns, and time is not fundamental—the universe can be understood as \textbf{static information}.
There is no ontological generation, no external clock, no metaphysical “creation.”

Consider \(n\) bits of information. These bits can be arranged in \(2^n\) distinct configurations. Each configuration encodes a possible universe.

\textbf{Time} emerges internally: it is the ordinal sequence of configurations experienced by an observer.
Observers traverse the configuration space according to the \textbf{order of experience}, not because the universe “runs” like a program.

\section{Defining the Observer}

An \textbf{observer} is a coherent informational pattern capable of sustaining continuity of experience. Formally:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Let \(\Gamma\) be the set of all configurations \(\gamma \in \Gamma\).
    \item Let \(\mathcal{O}(\gamma)\) be the number of observers in configuration \(\gamma\).
\end{itemize}

The probability that an observer finds themselves in a particular configuration is determined by the \textbf{joint measure of spectral, geometric, and observer-specific complexity}:

\[
\mathbb{P}(\gamma \mid O) = \frac{1}{Z_O} \exp\Big[-\lambda\, \mathcal{C}_O[\gamma]\Big], \quad \gamma \in \Gamma_O
\]

with 

\[
\mathcal{C}_O[\gamma] = C_Q[\gamma] + C_G[\gamma] + C_\text{obs}[\gamma; O].
\]

Here:

\begin{itemize}
\item \(C_Q[\gamma]\) = \textbf{spectral complexity} (microstructure compression, i.e., wave-like correlations).  
\item \(C_G[\gamma]\) = \textbf{geometric complexity} (macroscopic spacetime compression, i.e., curvature, continuity).  
\item \(C_\text{obs}[\gamma; O]\) = \textbf{observer continuity cost} (internal boundaries and consistency).  
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Key idea:} the most probable universes are those that \textbf{maximize the number of observers} while minimizing total complexity.
Observers don’t “choose” a universe—they simply exist in the highly probable configurations where patterns like them dominate.

\section{Spectral Compression: Why the Microcosmos Waves}

At the microscopic scale, the universe is best represented in the \textbf{spectral basis}:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Smooth, correlated structures compress efficiently with few spectral components.
    \item Sharp discontinuities or high-frequency fluctuations require many components and are exponentially improbable.
\end{itemize}

This explains:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Quantum waves and interference
    \item Linear superposition
    \item Smooth, predictable microphysics
\end{itemize}

Formally, the \textbf{spectral complexity} \(C_Q\) is the minimal number of frequency components needed to encode an observer-compatible history. Histories with low \(C_Q\) dominate the observer-conditioned measure:

\[
C_Q = \sum_i k_\text{eff}^2 |A_i|^2, \quad k_\text{eff} = \min(|k_i|, N-|k_i|).
\]

\section{Geometric Compression: Why Spacetime Exists}

Observers require well-defined internal boundaries to preserve continuity:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Memories, causal chains, and predictions must remain coherent.  
    \item Enforcing these constraints purely spectrally is expensive; the basis functions span the entire microcosmos.
\end{itemize}

Thus, macroscopic \textbf{geometry emerges} as the optimal compression of relational structure:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Curved spacetime encodes constraints efficiently.  
    \item Geodesic motion minimizes description length.  
    \item The Einstein-Hilbert action naturally represents geometric compression \(C_G\).  
\end{itemize}

Observers experience 3+1D spacetime because it is the \textbf{most efficient representation of relational continuity}.

\section{Joint Compression Principle}

The most probable configurations simultaneously minimize \textbf{spectral} and \textbf{geometric complexity}, while preserving \textbf{observer continuity}:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Microstructure = spectral compression  
    \item Macroscopic arena = geometric compression  
    \item Observer integrity = internal “inside” property
\end{itemize}

The joint complexity functional:

\[
\mathcal{C}_O[\gamma] = C_Q[\gamma] + C_G[\gamma] + C_\text{obs}[\gamma; O]
\]

dictates the \textbf{observer-conditioned probability}.

\textbf{Consequences:}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Smooth quantum waves and interference emerge naturally.  
    \item Geodesic motion, energy-momentum conservation, and general relativity arise as statistical consequences of geometric compression.  
    \item Observers always find themselves in configurations that preserve their internal structure.  
    \item Rare or high-frequency deviations (e.g., sudden death, collisions) are suppressed but possible with small probability.
\end{itemize}

Even a \textbf{tiny violation of internal boundaries} is disastrous for the observer: any intersection with an incompatible configuration destroys the pattern. The “inside” property is therefore \textbf{critical} for probability dominance.
