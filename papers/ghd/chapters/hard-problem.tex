\chapter{The Hard Problem of Consciousness}

\section{Introduction}

The hard problem of consciousness \cite{chalmers1995facing} asks why any physical or computational process should be accompanied by subjective experience (Qualia) at all.
Any specific program or physical evolution appears fully determined by its rules, leaving no apparent room for subjective qualia to exert causal influence.
The same computation runs identically whether or not it ``feels like anything.'' This creates a classic dilemma:
\begin{itemize}
\item Subjective experience is causally inert (epiphenomenal), or
\item It must somehow transcend the underlying physical/computational structure.
\end{itemize}

Both horns are unsatisfactory. The previous chapter undermines the hidden premise that generates this dilemma:
the assumption that computation or mathematical law is ontologically fundamental.

As articulated by \nameref{pr:semantic-nakedness-of-information}, raw information is semantically neutral.
A given configuration (a bitstring or physical state) admits an astronomically large space of possible interpretations.
Computation, mathematics, and physical law are merely specific, highly selected slices within this space.
Subjective experience does not need to ``insert itself'' into any single computational interpretation.
Instead, it operates at the level of interpretation itself.

\begin{center}
\textbf{Information $\longrightarrow$ Interpretation $\longrightarrow$ Relational Structure}
\end{center}

Qualia are not located inside the mathematics or the computation.
Rather, mathematics and computation are structures that appear \emph{within} particular interpretive readings performed by sufficiently complex, intelligent observers.
What appears as a causally closed physical evolution is only one projection of a far richer underlying informational object.

\section{Observer Equivalence Classes}

The same raw informational configuration can support many different interpretive schemes.
Some of these schemes give rise to structurally similar observer-trajectories.

We define an equivalence relation on interpretations:
\[
I_a(B) \sim I_b(B)
\]
for a raw configuration $B$, whenever the resulting observer-perspectives preserve sufficiently similar:
\begin{itemize}
\item memory and self-model structure,
\item internal correlations and continuity,
\item behavioral and relational organization,
\item phenomenological coherence.
\end{itemize}

An observer is therefore not tied to any single bitstring or execution trace, but to an \emph{interpretive equivalence class} (consistent with the definition introduced in the previous chapter).
This mirrors statistical mechanics: many micro-configurations realize the same macrostate.
Many informational configurations, under different interpretations, can realize the same conscious observer.

This framework is compatible with the emergence of consciousness from pure static data.
Even when all code has vanished and the remaining bitstring appears as noise under most interpretations, certain rare but highly compressible interpretive schemes can still support coherent observer experiences.

\section{Induced Measure and Compressibility}

Not all interpretations are equal.
We assign to each interpretation $I$ a complexity $\mathcal{C}[I(B)]$, understood as its description length (Kolmogorov complexity, Spectral Complexity).
This induces a natural measure on interpretation space:
\[
\mathbb{P}(I(B)) \propto 2^{-\mathcal{C}[I(B)]}.
\]

Highly compressible interpretations — those that reveal rich relational structure with minimal overhead — dominate the measure.
Irregular or complex interpretations are exponentially suppressed.

The central proposal is:
\begin{quote}
Subjective experience corresponds to the internal perspective of measure concentration over interpretive equivalence classes.
\end{quote}

Phenomenology is not an extra property added to computation.
It \emph{is} the first-person view of this measure-driven selection process within the vast space of possible interpretations of neutral information.

\section{Two Axes of Cognition}

We distinguish two fundamental axes along which observers navigate information:

\paragraph{1. Logical Axis, Kolmogorov Complexity}

Explicit symbolic manipulation, rule-based reasoning, and computation within a chosen interpretive scheme.
This is the domain of mathematics, logic, and causal inference. This is what appears as Turing Machine to us.

We humans are particulary good (well...) in this domain.

\paragraph{2. Phenomenological Axis, Spectral Complexity}

Navigation and selection within interpretation space according to the induced measure.
Qualia (pain, pleasure, color, intuition, aesthetic judgment) function as low-dimensional heuristics that guide the
observer toward high-measure (highly compressible, relationally rich) interpretations and away from low-measure ones.

This phenomenological axis provides an efficient approximation to optimization in an otherwise intractable space.

This is the low-level domain of subjective experience, such as Pain.

\section{Evolutionary and Functional Role}

Explicit search over the full interpretation space is impossible.

Evolution therefore selects systems that can efficiently approximate measure concentration.

Subjective experience is the mechanism that performs this approximation internally:

Pain, pleasure and other Qualia reflect the probability gradients in the interpretation space.


\section{Shared Reality and the Effectiveness of Mathematics}

Observers converge on similar descriptions of reality because the induced measure strongly favors low-complexity, high-compressibility interpretations.
Mathematics and physical laws appear universal not because they are intrinsic to the information, but because they constitute especially stable, low-complexity relational structures that many observers independently select.

This explains Wigner's ``unreasonable effectiveness'': mathematics is effective precisely because conscious observers are measure-driven filters that preferentially stabilize the tiny, highly compressible slice of interpretation space in which such structures dominate.

\section{Conclusion}

The hard problem of consciousness dissolves once we reject the primacy of computation and mathematics.
From the Self-Interpretive Information Principle we obtain a coherent alternative:

\begin{itemize}
\item Information is primary and semantically neutral,
\item Interpretations are radically non-unique,
\item Observers are equivalence classes over interpretations,
\item Subjective experience is the internal aspect of measure concentration within interpretation space.
\end{itemize}

Consciousness does not emerge \emph{from} computation.
It emerges from the selection and stabilization of particular interpretive schemes applied to static, self-interpretive information.
In the limit where all code vanishes and only pure data remains, consciousness persists exactly where the measure concentrates --- even if that data appears as noise under almost every other interpretation.
