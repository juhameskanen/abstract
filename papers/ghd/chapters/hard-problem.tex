\chapter{The Hard Problem as Observer-Measure Selection}

\section{Abstract}

This paper presents an informational framework in which subjective experience arises from observer-relative measure over interpretations of static information, rather than from computation or functional organization per se. 

Building on a block-universe ontology with a timeless wavefunction (Wheeler-DeWitt-like), we argue that deterministic computation is interpretation-relative and causally closed.
It does not generate phenomenology.
Instead, conscious experience is identified with the induced measure concentration over admissible observer-consistent interpretations of the underlying informational structure. 

Compressibility, formalized via joint spectral and geometric complexity (detailed in the companion paper \cite{meskanen2020}), defines a smooth measure over interpretation space.
Phenomenological valence (pain, pleasure, beauty, intuition) corresponds to local gradients and concentrations within this measure.
The framework dissolves the Hard Problem by identifying first-person experience directly with observer-relative measure selection rather than deriving it from physical or computational processes.

\section{Introduction}

The Hard Problem of consciousness \cite{chalmers1995facing} asks why any physical or computational process is accompanied by subjective experience.
Standard computational and functional theories successfully account for reportability, integration, and behavior, yet leave the existence of ``what it is like'' unexplained.

This paper rejects the usual framing. The central claim is:

\begin{quotation}
  Computation itself does not feel like anything.
  Subjective experience is not generated by the execution of code, but arises from the observer-relative selection of interpretations within a static informational structure.
\end{quotation}

In a timeless block universe, all configurations exist simultaneously.
Observers correspond to equivalence classes of high-measure interpretations of this structure.
Phenomenology is identical to the internal view of measure concentration under joint spectral-geometric complexity.

\section{Static Information and Interpretation Relativity}
Consider a finite (or effectively finite) informational structure $B$ representing a slice or global configuration of the block universe. An interpretation is a mapping
\[
I : B \mapsto \mathcal{M},
\]
where $\mathcal{M}$ may be a computational history, a geometric manifold, an observer trajectory, or a physical law.

Crucially, no interpretation is privileged at the ontological level.
The distinction between program and data, syntax and semantics, or execution and observation is relative to a chosen $I$.
Deterministic computation exists only *within* a fixed interpretation and is therefore causally closed under that interpretation.
There is no room left inside the computation for additional subjective properties.

Subjective experience must therefore reside elsewhere: in the selection and weighting of interpretations themselves.

\section{Observer Equivalence Classes}
Different interpretations $I_a$ and $I_b$ may describe approximately equivalent observers.
We define an equivalence relation $I_a \sim I_b$ when the induced observer trajectories preserve:
\begin{itemize}
    \item memory and informational continuity,
    \item geometric coherence,
    \item phenomenological stability.
\end{itemize}

Observers are thus not single execution traces but equivalence classes of interpretations (realized, in concrete models, as Gaussian blobs in configuration space with characteristic spectral signatures). Consciousness is not binary but graded, with measure concentration determining the ``strength'' or richness of experience.

\section{Induced Measure via Spectral-Geometric Complexity}

As developed in the companion paper \cite{meskanen2020}, we replace discrete Kolmogorov complexity with a joint spectral-geometric complexity measure $\mathcal{C}[I(B)]$.
This yields a smooth, well-behaved probability measure over interpretation space:
\[
\mathbb{P}(I) \propto \exp\left(-\mathcal{C}[I(B)]\right).
\]

High-compressibility interpretations (those exhibiting strong spectral and geometric regularities) dominate the measure.
Low-compressibility interpretations are exponentially suppressed.

\textbf{Central Proposal:}  
Subjective experience \emph{is} the first-person perspective of an observer equivalence class under this induced measure.
Phenomenology is identical to observer-relative measure concentration. There is no further emergence step required.

This identification directly addresses the Hard Problem: the question ``why should information processing feel like anything?'' is replaced by ``what else could first-person existence possibly be, other than the internal aspect of high-measure interpretive selection in a static universe?''

\section{Two Axes of Cognition}

We distinguish two informational axes:

\paragraph{1. Logical Axis (Code-like)}  
Explicit symbolic reasoning, formal computation, causal inference, and planning. This axis is interpretation-relative and does not intrinsically generate feeling. It corresponds to what we subjectively experience as ``thinking.''

\paragraph{2. Phenomenological Axis (Measure Geometry)}  
Navigation and sensitivity to gradients in the induced measure landscape. Pain, pleasure, intuition, and aesthetic experience arise here as responses to:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Positive gradients in complexity ($\Delta \mathcal{C} > 0$) $\rightarrow$ fragmentation, instability, pain.
    \item Negative gradients ($\Delta \mathcal{C} < 0$) $\rightarrow$ compression, symmetry, unification, pleasure/beauty.
\end{itemize}

Intuition and aesthetic sense function as low-dimensional projections of high-dimensional compressibility optimization.

\section{Evolutionary and Functional Role}
In a timeless ontology, evolution selects for observers whose equivalence classes are high-measure.
Subjective experience provides an efficient heuristic for navigating interpretation space: pain steers away from low-measure (incoherent) trajectories, pleasure reinforces high-compressibility ones. This explains why phenomenology is adaptive without violating the causal closure of any particular computational interpretation.

\section{Free Will and Shared Reality}
Within any fixed interpretation, dynamics may be deterministic.
Global freedom arises because the observer is not bound to a single interpretation.
The experienced ``self'' samples across high-measure equivalence classes.

Shared mathematics and physical law emerge naturally: observers converge on the most compressible (highest-measure) interpretive structures.
Universal arithmetic and geometry are attractors in interpretation space, not Platonic entities.

\section{Relation to the Hard Problem}

Conventional theories attempt to derive experience from neural activity, information integration, or computation.
The present framework inverts this: computation and physical law are secondary, arising only relative to chosen interpretations.
Phenomenology is primary — it is the direct manifestation of measure concentration for self-interpreting observers.

By giving up the assumption of a privileged computational interpretation, the Hard Problem becomes tractable.
Computation is blind. Experience is what it is like to \emph{be} a high-measure observer in interpretation space.

\section{Open Problems and Empirical Predictions}
A full treatment appears in the main paper \cite{meskanen2020}. Key remaining challenges include:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Rigorous definition and dynamics of observer equivalence classes (Gaussian blobs in spectral-geometric space).
    \item Quantitative modeling of specific qualia (color, taste, etc.) beyond valence.
    \item Explicit mapping from the timeless wavefunction to experienced time.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Empirical directions:} The theory predicts that disruptions to spectral-geometric coherence (e.g., certain anesthetics, psychedelics, or pathological states)
should produce measurable shifts in compressibility signatures that correlate with reported changes in phenomenal intensity and valence, beyond mere functional impairment.

\section{Conclusion}

Consciousness does not emerge from computation. Computation is a shadow cast by high-measure interpretations.
In a static block universe, observers are compressible self-interpretive structures selected by induced spectral-geometric measure.
Subjective experience is the internal geometry of that selection process.

This framework offers a unified route toward resolving the Hard Problem without epiphenomenalism or strong emergence, while remaining compatible with a fundamental observer-centric physics.
