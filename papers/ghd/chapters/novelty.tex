\section{The Block Universe of Information}

A static universe where the totality of existence is simultaneous and invariant is known as the Block Universe.
However, unlike the traditional relativistic "loaf" of spacetime, this framework proposes a Spectral Block: a compressed,
multidimensional substrate of pure information.

By removing time ($t$) from the fundamental equation, we arrive at a truly timeless state. The "Universal Wavefunction" does not evolve; it simply \textit{is}.
The appearance of the temporal arrow is merely an ordinal index of configurations.

In standard Everettian (Many-Worlds) interpretations, the universe splits into a branching tree of realities.
In this framework, no such splitting occurs. Instead, all possible histories coexist as distinct "tracks" or spectral modes
within the same underlying informational substrate. We do not branch into a new universe; we simply navigate a path of high probability
through a pre-existing worlds. All worlds are already present in the block; our consciousness merely traces a coherent trajectory through them.

\subsection*{Beyond the Wavefunction: Representation Length}

The departure from traditional Wheeler-DeWitt theory lies in the introduction of Representation Length ($K_{rep}$).
This provides the selection mechanism that explains why we inhabit a structured universe rather than a chaotic soup.
A chaotic universe lacks spectral regularity; its representation cost is massive, rendering its weight in the induced measure near zero. 

The orderly universes—those characterized by 3+1 dimensions and stable physical laws—represent the global compression peaks.
These configurations are the most efficient ways to arrange $n$ bits of information. We do not live in a stable world by accident,
but because stability is the most probable state for a compressed observer.


\section{The Static Universe and the Problem of Time}

The \textbf{Wheeler-DeWitt equation} ($H\Psi = 0$) has long suggested a ``frozen'' universe—a static wavefunction where time is not a fundamental parameter but an internal correlation. For the average programmer, this is the ``DVD analogy'': every scene of the movie exists simultaneously on the disc. However, there is no external laser to read the movie. Time only ``happens'' when actors in the movie realize they are alive, can think, reason and make choices to stay alive and play their roles thorugh the frames.

Our theory accepts this static ontology and appears to solve the \textbf{Selection Problem}. While Wheeler-DeWitt tells us the universe is a static state, it does not explain why we perceive \textit{this} specific, law-like history among infinite chaotic possibilities. By introducing the \textbf{Solomonoff Prior}, we replace functional differential equations with a combinatorial measure over bitstrings. We inhabit this specific history because it is the most \textbf{compressible} (and thus most probable) path through the static ensemble.
