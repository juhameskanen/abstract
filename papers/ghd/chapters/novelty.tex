In standard Everettian (Many-Worlds) interpretations, the universe splits into a branching tree of realities.
In this framework, no such splitting occurs. Instead, all possible histories coexist as distinct "tracks" or spectral modes
within the same underlying informational substrate. We do not branch into a new universe; we simply navigate a path of high probability
through a pre-existing worlds. All worlds are already present in the block; our consciousness merely traces a coherent trajectory through them.

\subsection*{Beyond the Wavefunction: Representation Length}

The departure from traditional Wheeler-DeWitt theory lies in the introduction of Representation Length ($K_{rep}$).
This provides the selection mechanism that explains why we inhabit a structured universe rather than a chaotic soup.
A chaotic universe lacks spectral regularity; its representation cost is massive, rendering its weight in the induced measure near zero. 

The orderly universes—those characterized by $3+1$ dimensions and stable physical laws—represent the global compression peaks.
These configurations are the most efficient ways to arrange $n$ bits of information. We do not live in a stable world by accident,
but because stability is the most probable state for a compressed observer.
