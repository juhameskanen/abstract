\begin{abstract}
Papers~I--V established that the universe is a finite static bitstring
of $n$ bits; that gravitational collapse converges to a
zero-entropy singularity; that expansion is the geometric reading of
entropy increase; that the aspect ratio of spacetime is independent of
$n$; and that a relational scale factor $R(t)$ emerges from strict
information conservation via the counting equation
$R(t) = (\rho_{\text{fabric}}(t) + k(t))/n$.
The present paper applies this framework at cosmological scale, now
supported by a parameter-light multi-clock simulation. It establishes
three results. First, the three-phase expansion profile of standard
cosmology (early rapid growth, matter-driven deceleration, late-time
re-expansion) emerges when matter abundance follows
hump-shaped curves derived exactly from the Ehrenfest process
acting on finite-width bit patterns, with no cosmological constant,
inflaton, or dark energy term introduced. This macroscopic profile is
verified numerically to be independent of $n$ once expressed in
normalized bit-flip time. Second, the informational level hierarchy
$L_0 \supset L_{\rm DM} \supset L_\nu \supset L_{\rm bar}$ provides a
natural mechanism for dark matter. Third, hump-shaped abundance is not
a universal property of arbitrary filters -- it is the generic outcome
for filters whose required-zero count exceeds their required-one count
($a<b$ in the notation below) -- and the ensemble of such filters
dominates the typical behavior of a finite window, which is what the
simulation's structure-fraction actually measures.

The background relaxation dynamics are genuinely
parameter-free ($k_{\rm rate}=2$ is derived from the Ehrenfest process,
not fit). Two open, non-derived choices remain in the cosmological
application specifically -- the late-time evaporation exponent and the
particle bit-widths -- and are stated as such in
Section~\ref{sec:future} rather than folded into the headline claims.
Finally, this paper's classical bitstring model is
explicitly framed (Section~\ref{sec:shadow}) as a statistical shadow
of the framework's deeper $\psi$-layer content, per the D--$\psi$--G
ordering, rather than as a fundamental description in its own right;
its genuine contribution is the demonstration that the three-phase
profile is a typical, untuned outcome, not a claim that this classical
layer is where the fundamental physics resides.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The present paper addresses two questions that standard cosmology
answers with separate, independently postulated mechanisms: why does
the universe expand in three phases (early inflationary growth,
matter-driven deceleration, late-time acceleration), and what is dark
matter?

In $\Lambda$CDM, late-time acceleration requires a cosmological
constant $\Lambda$ whose physical origin remains unexplained; dark
matter requires a new particle species whose nature has not been
determined after decades of direct-detection effort.

The present paper shows that both phenomena are consequences of the
single informational counting principle established in Papers~I--V.
No cosmological constant and no new particle species are postulated.
The cosmological constant and dark matter both dissolve into the
level structure of the bit budget -- with the caveats on evaporation
and bit-widths noted in Section~\ref{sec:future} made explicit rather
than assumed away.

The paper is structured as follows.
Section~\ref{sec:hierarchy} establishes the informational level
hierarchy and identifies dark matter with the first emergent level.
Section~\ref{sec:bosons} states the boson mechanism that makes
$L_{\rm DM}$ electromagnetically invisible.
Section~\ref{sec:hump} recalls the exact hump-classification result
from the Entropy and Emergent Structures wiki and applies it to each
level, replacing the earlier lognormal-fit treatment.
Section~\ref{sec:expansion} derives the three-phase expansion profile
and reports what the corrected multi-clock simulation actually shows,
including where it departs from the idealized picture.
Section~\ref{sec:data} discusses the relationship to $\Lambda$CDM
observational data.
Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} states the three foundational results.
Section~\ref{sec:future} identifies open problems, now including the
items uncovered during simulation verification.

\section{The Informational Level Hierarchy}
\label{sec:hierarchy}

The bit budget $n$ is partitioned at each moment between the
spacetime fabric $L_0$ (free, independently addressable bits) and a
nested sequence of composite structures $L_{\rm DM}, L_\nu, L_{\rm bar},
\ldots$ formed by the entropy gradient $dS/dt$.
The partition is exact at every tick:

\begin{equation}
  n = \rho_{\text{fabric}}(t) + \sum_k w_k \cdot N_k(t),
  \label{eq:budget}
\end{equation}

where $w_k$ is the bit-width of a level-$k$ entity and $N_k(t)$ is
its instantaneous count. Information is strictly conserved; no bits
enter or leave the system. Composite structures are composed from the
same bit-budget as the spacetime fabric. Therefore, their emergence
reduces the observable resolution of the universe, as viewed by
internal observers.

The four levels relevant at cosmological scale are:

\begin{description}
  \item[$L_0$ --- Spacetime fabric.]
    Bits not consumed by any composite structure.
    This is the rope of Paper~IV: its length at time $t$ is
    $\rho_{\text{fabric}}(t) = S(t) \cdot n - m(t)$, where
    $S(t) = I_{\max}(1 - e^{-kt})$ is the total unfolded entropy
    and $m(t) = \sum_k w_k N_k(t)$ is the total bits consumed by
    structures.

  \item[$L_{\rm DM}$ --- Dark matter.]
    The first-order condensates of the entropy gradient: the earliest
    and simplest structures to emerge as $dS/dt$ rises from zero.
    Whether $L_{\rm DM}$'s own composition falls in the hump-producing
    class ($a<b$; Section~\ref{sec:hump}) has not been independently
    derived from first principles -- it is currently supported by
    the ensemble argument that hump-producing compositions dominate
    typical windows of the relevant width. 
    Their bit-width $w_{\rm DM}$ is smaller than that of hadronic
    structures.

  \item[$L_\nu$ --- Neutrinos.]
    Second-order structures, emerging later than $L_{\rm DM}$ along
    the entropy gradient.

  \item[$L_{\rm bar}$ --- Baryons.]
    Third-order structures (protons, neutrons), emerging later still.
    Higher composite states (atoms, molecules) are relabellings of the
    same baryon number and do not enter the counting equation
    separately: a water molecule withdraws the same number of bits
    from $L_0$ as its constituent hadrons, because it is the same
    bits restructured, not new bits consumed.
\end{description}

The relational scale factor is:

\begin{equation}
  R(t) = \frac{\rho_{\text{fabric}}(t) + k(t)}{n},
  \label{eq:scalefactor}
\end{equation}

where $k(t) = \sum_k N_k(t)$ is the total count of composite entities
(each counts as one addressable token, regardless of its bit-width).

\section{Why Dark Matter is Dark: Bosons as Compression Residuals}
\label{sec:bosons}

In the \theory{} framework there is no electromagnetic force as a
separately postulated interaction. Bosons --- including photons ---
are compression residuals: the information shed at the boundary of a
fermionic compression event. When a fermion structure forms, it
compresses a region of the bitstring; the bits that cannot be absorbed
into the compressed structure are emitted as a boson. The boson is
the exhaust of the compression, not a mediator exchanged between
pre-existing charges.

This framing has a direct consequence for dark matter. $L_{\rm DM}$
structures are first-order condensates: they form earlier, from a
simpler compression template, than the $L_\nu$ and $L_{\rm bar}$
structures that give rise to photon-type residuals.
An $L_{\rm DM}$ compression event sheds residuals of a different
symmetry class --- or no residual detectable by an $L_{\rm bar}$-level
instrument at all --- because the compression geometry that produces
photon-type bosons has not yet emerged at that level of the hierarchy.

Consequently:

\begin{itemize}
  \item $L_{\rm DM}$ gravitates by the counting equation
    (equation~\ref{eq:scalefactor}): it withdraws bits from $L_0$,
    reducing $\rho_{\text{fabric}}(t)$, which an internal observer
    reads as curvature. This is identical to how $L_{\rm bar}$
    gravitates; the mechanism is level-independent.
  \item $L_{\rm DM}$ does not interact with $L_{\rm bar}$-level
    detectors electromagnetically, not because it is ``hidden'' or
    ``weakly coupled'', but because the boson residuals it sheds do
    not belong to the compression class that $L_{\rm bar}$ instruments
    are constituted to register.
\end{itemize}

This is not a new postulate. It follows from the boson-as-residual
principle established in Papers~VIII--XI for the particle spectrum,
applied to the cosmological hierarchy. The ``darkness'' of dark matter
is a structural consequence of its level, not a property requiring
separate explanation.

\section{Hump-Shaped Matter Abundance}
\label{sec:hump}

\subsection{The exact closed form}

For the symmetric Ehrenfest bit-flip process (start all-zero, flip a
uniformly random bit each tick), each bit is independently 1 with
probability, in normalized bit-flip time $\tau = \text{flip
count}/n$:

\begin{equation}
  p(\tau) = \tfrac12\left(1-e^{-2\tau}\right).
  \label{eq:ptau}
\end{equation}

This is a derived consequence of the Ehrenfest dynamics, not a fitted
curve; $k_{\rm rate}=2$ in this normalization is forced by
$dp/d\tau = 1-2p$, the mean-field equation of the process itself.

For a fixed bit-pattern of length $L=a+b$ ($a$ required ones, $b$
required zeros), the probability of that exact pattern occurring at a
random position is, in the independent-bit continuum limit:

\begin{equation}
  P(\text{pattern}\mid\tau) = p(\tau)^a\,(1-p(\tau))^b.
  \label{eq:patternprob}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Classification: three shapes, not one}

The shape of equation~(\ref{eq:patternprob}) is
fully determined, in advance, by the sign of $a-b$:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Composition & Shape & Peak \\
\midrule
$a>b$ & monotonic rise, no peak & unreachable ($p^*>0.5$) \\
$a=b$ & monotonic rise, flattens at boundary & at $p^*=0.5$ (the edge) \\
$a<b$ & rises, peaks, declines to plateau & genuine interior hump \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Only the $a<b$ class produces a hump. For that class, the peak
location, amplitude, and tail behavior are exact:

\begin{align}
  p^* &= \frac{a}{a+b}, \qquad
  \tau^* = -\tfrac12\ln(1-2p^*), \\
  \frac{\text{peak}}{\text{plateau}} &= 2^{\,L(1-H_2(p^*))},
\end{align}

where $H_2(q)=-q\log_2 q-(1-q)\log_2(1-q)$. The tail approaches its
plateau as $e^{-2\tau}$ for $a\neq b$ (half the exponent of entropy's
own approach to maximum, $e^{-4\tau}$, since entropy sits at a flat
quadratic extremum at $p=1/2$ while a generic asymmetric pattern does
not) and as $e^{-4\tau}$ for the symmetric $a=b$ case. Critically, the
plateau is \emph{nonzero}: an $a<b$ pattern does not vanish at
equilibrium, it approaches a fixed residual probability
$(0.5)^L$-scale.

\subsection{What the simulation actually measures}

The multi-clock simulation does not evaluate one fixed $(a,b)$
pattern. For a window of width $w$, it classifies the window's
ones-count $K$ (exact finite-population hypergeometric, not the
independent-bit approximation above) against the threshold $w/2$:
$K=0$ (``fall''), $0<K<w/2$ (``bump''), $K\ge w/2$ (``rise''). Since
$K<w/2$ is exactly the $a<b$ condition, the simulation's ``bump''
fraction is the aggregated probability mass over every hump-producing
composition of that window, and ``rise'' is the aggregated non-hump
class. Numerically, this aggregate is a single, unimodal hump for
every width tested, and dominates (bump fraction $0.66$--$0.9999$
across $w=6,12,20,40$). 

\subsection{Status of the hump hypothesis for each level}

\begin{description}
  \item[$L_{\rm DM}$.]
    \textbf{Supported by ensemble typicality, not yet a guaranteed
    prediction.} The aggregate bump fraction is hump-shaped and
    dominant for the widths tested. Whether $L_{\rm DM}$'s specific,
    physically realized composition satisfies $a<b$ has not been
    derived independently -- see Section~\ref{sec:future}.

  \item[$L_\nu$, $L_{\rm bar}$.]
    \textbf{Hypothesis under test.} $\Lambda$CDM predicts
    monotonically diluting number densities $n(t) = n_0(1+z(t))^3$ for
    both species after their respective freeze-out epochs: no rise, no
    peak, no fall, and no nonzero late-time plateau. The hump form
    above is a genuinely different, falsifiable prediction: it implies
    a nonzero relic abundance persisting indefinitely, rather than a
    density diluting toward zero. Section~\ref{sec:data} discusses
    what an honest falsifiability test would require.
\end{description}

\section{The Three-Phase Expansion Profile}
\label{sec:expansion}

\subsection{The mechanism}

Three phases emerge from $R(t)$ (equation~\ref{eq:scalefactor}) when
matter abundance follows the hump form of Section~\ref{sec:hump}.

\textbf{Phase 1 --- Early rapid expansion.}
Before significant matter formation, $\rho_{\text{fabric}}(t) \approx
S(t)\cdot n$ and $R(t)\approx S(t)$: the De~Sitter regime, tracking
the entropy saturation curve. No inflaton field is required.

\textbf{Phase 2 --- Matter-driven deceleration.}
As $L_{\rm DM}$, $L_\nu$, $L_{\rm bar}$ structures rise along their
hump curves, bits are withdrawn from $L_0$; $\rho_{\text{fabric}}(t)$
falls below $S(t)\cdot n$, read by an internal observer as
gravitational deceleration.

\textbf{Phase 3 --- Late-time re-expansion.}
As the hump curves decline from their peaks, bits are partially
released back to $L_0$; $\rho_{\text{fabric}}(t)$ partially recovers
and $R(t)$ rises again, read as accelerating expansion. This is
partial, not complete: Section~\ref{sec:hump} established that hump
curves plateau at a nonzero value rather than decaying to zero, so the
recovery in Phase 3 does not fully return the system to the pure
entropy envelope. A residual, non-vanishing matter fraction persists.
This is a substantive revision from the original version of this
paper, which described complete dissolution; the exact result supports
only partial dissolution to a nonzero relic, which is arguably the
more interesting and more falsifiable claim, since it forbids a
universe that returns exactly to the pure-entropy curve.

\subsection{Numerical Verification}

A multi-clock simulation (Ehrenfest substrate plus per-scale
retarded clocks, one tick per $w$ raw flips for a width-$w$ structure)
was used to check this mechanism directly rather than assume it.
Two results from that verification are reported here because they
affect what can honestly be claimed:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{The macroscopic three-phase envelope is independent of
    $n$.} Tested across $n=500$ to $n=128{,}000$ with fixed
    structure widths, the envelope of $R(t)$ (or its proxy,
    size\_measure), expressed in normalized time $\tau=\text{raw}/n$,
    converges to a stable shape for $n\gtrsim$ a few thousand: peak-matter
    timing and envelope values at fixed $\tau$ agree to better than
    1\% across two orders of magnitude in $n$. This paper's central
    claim -- three phases, no tuning -- does not depend on any specific
    calibrated value of $n$.
  \item \textbf{The exact curve is not as smooth as Figure~1
    implies.} With a small number of fixed-bit-width structure
    species (as used throughout this paper's illustrative figures),
    the combined budget curve shows persistent small-scale
    oscillation on top of the stable macro-envelope -- tens to
    thousands of local sign changes in its derivative, depending on
    $n$, that do \emph{not} vanish as $n\to\infty$ when the species'
    bit-widths are held fixed (as they should be, since a real
    particle's bit-width is not expected to scale with the size of the
    universe). Whether this reflects genuine physical discreteness
    from a small number of composite species, or is an artifact of the
    placeholder widths used before $E=mc^2$ supplies realistic ones,
    is open; see Section~\ref{sec:future}.
\end{enumerate}

The three-phase profile is therefore established as a macroscopic,
$n$-independent trend.

\section{Relationship to \texorpdfstring{$\Lambda$}{Lambda}CDM Observational Data}
\label{sec:data}

The $\Lambda$CDM number-density curves used as a reference baseline
for $L_\nu$ and $L_{\rm bar}$ in Figure~\ref{fig:expansion} are not
theory-independent observational facts. They are derived quantities
within a specific theoretical framework: $n(t) = n_0(1+z(t))^3$,
where $z(t)$ is obtained by integrating the Friedmann equation with
Planck~2018 parameters ($H_0 = 67.4$~km/s/Mpc, $\Omega_m = 0.315$,
$\Omega_\Lambda = 0.685$). Feeding these curves into the \theory{}
simulation tests the \theory{} framework against a $\Lambda$CDM
prediction, not against raw data.

The actual observational anchors that $\Lambda$CDM is itself fitted
to --- the CMB power spectrum, the Type~Ia supernova distance-redshift
relation, the baryon acoustic oscillation scale, the light element
abundances from BBN --- are what the \theory{} framework must
ultimately be tested against. A proper falsifiability test would
require:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Deriving $E = mc^2$ within the framework, to convert
    informational bit-counts to energy-momentum and thence to
    observable quantities.
  \item A revised time-calibration anchoring bit-flip ticks to
    physical time. The previous candidate for this
    (Paper~IV's aspect-ratio argument fixing $n\approx184$) has been
    withdrawn and is being redone; until it is replaced, this step is
    an open gap in the programme, not a solved step being reused here.
  \item Comparing the resulting $R(t)$ directly to the
    distance-redshift relation of the Type~Ia supernova datasets,
    not to $\Lambda$CDM's derived expansion history.
  \item Testing the nonzero-relic prediction of Section~\ref{sec:hump}
    against late-time/asymptotic dark matter density constraints,
    since this is a genuine point of disagreement with $\Lambda$CDM
    and does not require $E=mc^2$ to state qualitatively, only to
    quantify.
\end{enumerate}

This programme is identified as future work in
Section~\ref{sec:future}. The present paper establishes the
qualitative mechanism and the correct level hierarchy; the
quantitative confrontation with raw data awaits Steps~1--2.

\section{Status of This Model: A Statistical Shadow, Not a Foundation}
\label{sec:shadow}

Before stating the paper's conclusions, it is worth being explicit about
what kind of result this paper actually is.

The framework's own D--$\psi$--G ordering (Entropy and Emergent
Structures wiki; The Equivalence Class Structure of Physical Reality
wiki) treats the compressed, complex-valued codec $\psi$ as primitive:
bits ($D$) are a Born-rule sampled, discrete description of $\psi$, not
a substrate $\psi$ can be reconstructed from by aggregation, and
geometry ($G$) is what large-scale $\psi$ structure looks like from
inside. Standard physics builds upward from $D$; this framework argues
that direction is backwards.

The cosmological model of this paper operates entirely at the $D$
layer: a classical bitstring under Ehrenfest dynamics, with no complex
amplitudes, superposition, or coherence anywhere in its construction.
This was tested directly, not merely asserted: individual classical
hop events, encoded one at a time into Paper~VII's single-fermion
codec ($\psi = f_0 + if_1$), reproduce the exact universal boson norm
$\|B\|=1/\sqrt2$ to floating-point precision. But averaging classical
population statistics across many independent trials and treating the
resulting frequency as a Born-rule amplitude does \emph{not} reproduce
coherent structure -- the resulting density matrix is exactly diagonal,
with zero boson signal, in contrast to the nonzero signal a naive
pure-state ansatz would predict from the same numbers. Coherence is not
recoverable from classical ensemble statistics by averaging; this is a
direct, numerical instance of the D--$\psi$--G ordering's claim that
$D$ is sampled from $\psi$, not the other way around.

This paper's cosmological model should therefore be read as a
simplified \textbf{statistical shadow} of the framework's deeper
$\psi$-layer content, not as a fundamental description in its own
right. However, it shows that the qualitative
three-phase expansion history is a \emph{typical}, untuned outcome once
one assumes only information conservation and a finite-width filter --
no fine-tuning of a cosmological constant, an inflaton potential, or a
dark energy equation of state is required to get a universe that

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper establishes three results.

\textbf{First}, the three-phase expansion profile of standard
cosmology --- early rapid growth, matter-driven deceleration,
late-time re-expansion --- emerges from the relational scale factor
$R(t)$ when matter abundance follows the exact hump form of
Section~\ref{sec:hump}, with no cosmological constant, inflaton field,
or dark energy term. This macroscopic profile is verified to be
independent of $n$. The late-time re-expansion is partial, not
complete: the framework predicts a nonzero relic matter fraction
persisting indefinitely, which is a point of genuine disagreement with
$\Lambda$CDM rather than a re-derivation of it.

\textbf{Second}, dark matter is identified with the first-order
emergent level $L_{\rm DM}$ of the informational hierarchy. It
gravitates via the counting equation by exactly the same mechanism
as ordinary matter --- by withdrawing bits from the spacetime fabric
--- and is electromagnetically invisible not because it is weakly
coupled but because it sheds compression residuals of a different
symmetry class than the photon-type bosons that $L_{\rm bar}$-level
detectors are constituted to register.

\textbf{Third}, hump-shaped abundance is the generic, dominant outcome
for finite windows in a relaxing bitstring -- not a universal property
of every filter, but the typical one, since hump-producing
compositions ($a<b$) dominate the aggregate probability mass for the
widths tested. This predicts a disagreement with $\Lambda$CDM's monotonic
dilution.

\section{Open Problems}
\label{sec:future}

\subsection*{Derivation of $E = mc^2$}

The amplitude scaling between
informational bit-counts and physical energy-momentum is the critical
missing link, pursued in the discrete counting arena $\mathcal{C}_D$.


\subsection*{The late-time evaporation exponent is not derived}

The multi-clock simulation's matter definition reweights the exact
structure fraction by an order-parameter power, $\eta(\tau)^p$. This
exponent is not fixed by the combinatorics -- it is a free choice with
large physical consequences: $p=0$ (no reweighting, using the exact
bump fraction directly) leaves a substantial relic ($\sim$73\% of peak
matter survives to late times in the tested configuration); $p=1$
drives matter to near-total evaporation ($<0.01\%$ of peak survives).
Given Section~\ref{sec:hump}'s exact result that the underlying bump
fraction itself plateaus rather than vanishing, $p=0$ is closer to
what the bare combinatorics support, and is the better default absent
a derivation; $p=1$ was the earlier default and should not be treated
as the theory's prediction until an exponent is actually derived from
the model rather than chosen for its late-time behavior.

\subsection*{Structure bit-widths are placeholders}

The widths used in illustrative figures ($6$, $12$, $20$ bits) are not
derived from the actual $L_{\rm DM}/L_\nu/L_{\rm bar}$ spectrum; they
await the $E=mc^2$ derivation above. Preliminary checks show the
qualitative macro-envelope (Section~\ref{sec:expansion}) is not
sensitive to the specific placeholder values, but the fine-scale
discreteness noted there may be, and should be re-examined once
realistic widths are available.

\subsection*{The 5:1 Dark-Matter-to-Baryon Ratio}

Unchanged in substance from the previous version: if visible matter
forms from dark matter via the same hump mechanism applied
recursively, self-similarity would predict
$N_{\rm bar}/N_{\rm fabric} = (N_{\rm DM}/N_{\rm fabric})^2$, a
power-law relation between levels. The observed ratio
$\rho_{\rm DM}/\rho_{\rm bar}\approx5$ remains a target this relation
should reproduce once bit-widths and $E=mc^2$ are available; the
numerical coincidence with the lepton mass ladder's bit-counting
(Paper~VIII) and with the WIMP mass range is noted as suggestive, not
as a derived result.

\subsection*{Quantitative Confrontation with Raw Observational Anchors}

As discussed in Section~\ref{sec:data}, the proper falsifiability
test is a direct comparison of $R(t)$ to the Type~Ia supernova
distance-redshift relation and the CMB power spectrum. This requires
completing the $E=mc^2$ derivation and replacing the withdrawn time
anchor.
