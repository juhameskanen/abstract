\section*{Scope}
 
This note states only what the validated combinatorial model
(\texttt{emergent\_structure\_relativistic.py}) actually establishes about
spatial and temporal resolution. 
 
This note makes \textbf{no} claim about the numerical value of $n$, about
any specific coherence scale $w$, about black hole entropy, or about
inflationary or cosmological data. Those were either artifacts of the
retracted exponential argument or remain open problems requiring independent
derivations not attempted here. $n$ and $w$ are left as free, uncalibrated
symbols throughout.
 
\section{Setup}
 
The universe is a finite bitstring of length $n$, evolving under the
symmetric Ehrenfest process (one of the $n$ positions chosen uniformly and
flipped, at each raw tick). Its entropy per bit is
$S(\tau) = \log_2\binom{n}{k(\tau)}/n$, with $k(\tau)$ the exact ones-count
at bit-flip time $\tau$. The entropy-unfolded bit budget is
$n\cdot S(\tau)$, and the portion of it actually resolved into fabric and
non-decayed structure at time $\tau$ is $n\cdot R(\tau)$, where
$R(\tau) \in [0,1]$ is the size measure validated in the emergent-structure
script. Nothing here depends on which coherence scales, if any, are chosen
to decompose that structure further.
 
\section{Spatial Resolution}
 
The model works entirely in bit-count space, not configuration-count space:
$R(\tau)$ is a fraction of $n$, never of $2^n$. The honest spatial
resolution is therefore linear in $n$:
\begin{equation}
  \Delta x(\tau) = R(\tau)\cdot n \cdot \ell_P,
  \qquad
  \Delta x_{\max} = n\,\ell_P,
  \label{eq:dxmax}
\end{equation}
the second equality holding in the limit $\tau\to\infty$, where matter has
fully decayed and $R(\tau)\to S(\tau)\to 1$. 
 
\section{Temporal Resolution}
 
There is no single temporal resolution. The
model supports two distinct, non-interchangeable notions.
 
\subsection{Global relaxation time (substrate-level)}
 
The time for the whole $n$-bit string's Ehrenfest process to reach
statistical equilibrium is the mixing time of the random walk on the
$n$-cube: flipping one uniformly chosen coordinate per step has a total-variation
cutoff at $\frac{n}{2}\ln n$ steps \cite{diaconis1996}. Identifying one raw
tick with one Planck time:
\begin{equation}
  \Delta t_{\rm relax}(n) = \frac{n}{2}\ln n \cdot t_P.
  \label{eq:trelax}
\end{equation}

 
\subsection{Per-structure proper-time lapse}
 
Any emergent structure encoded in $w$ bits is blind to anything finer than
$w$ raw flips: it ticks its own clock once per $w$ raw ticks. This is a
general rule holding for arbitrary $w\in\{1,\dots,n\}$, not a claim about
any particular, physically privileged scale:
\begin{equation}
  \Delta t_w = w\cdot t_P,
  \qquad
  N_w = \frac{1}{w} \quad\text{(lapse)}.
  \label{eq:lapse}
\end{equation}
 
\section{Aspect Ratios}
 
Two aspect ratios follow, corresponding to the two temporal notions above.
Both are stated as functions of the free symbols $n$ (and $w$, where
relevant) — no value is substituted.
 
\subsection{Global aspect ratio}
 
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{A}_{\rm global}(n) = \frac{\Delta x_{\max}}{\Delta t_{\rm relax}}
  = \frac{n\,\ell_P}{\tfrac{n}{2}\ln n\cdot t_P}
  = \frac{2}{\ln n}\cdot c.
  \label{eq:aglobal}
\end{equation}
 
\begin{lemma}[Monotonicity]
$\mathcal{A}_{\rm global}(n)$ is strictly decreasing for $n > 1$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
$\dfrac{d}{dn}\dfrac{2}{\ln n} = -\dfrac{2}{n(\ln n)^2} < 0$ for all $n>1$.
\end{proof}
 
This is a structural
consequence of working in linear bit-count space, not a numerical
adjustment.
 
\subsection{Per-scale aspect ratio}
 
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{A}_w(n) = \frac{\Delta x_{\max}}{\Delta t_w} = \frac{n}{w}\cdot c,
  \qquad 1 \le w \le n.
  \label{eq:aw}
\end{equation}
 
\begin{corollary}[Boundary cases]
At $w = n$ (the coarsest admissible structure, the whole string acting as
a single unit), $\mathcal{A}_n(n) = c$ exactly, independent of $n$. At
$w = 1$ (the finest admissible structure, a single bit),
$\mathcal{A}_1(n) = n\cdot c$.
\end{corollary}
 
The $w=n$ case is an unforced identity — it requires no fitted constant,
no $4\pi$-type correction, and holds for every $n$. It is the one place in
this note where a dimensionless constant of exactly 1 (in units of $c$)
appears without being put in by hand.
