\begin{abstract}
We investigate black hole singularities from an information-theoretic
perspective within the \iame{} framework. Under the Internal Emergence
Principle established in Paper~I, geometric spacetime and the execution
trace of a computational simulation are two equivalent representations of
the same underlying informational structure, with neither being more
fundamental. Standard General Relativity cannot be evolved through
singularity formation due to numerical divergences and the breakdown of
the manifold description. The execution trace provides a complementary,
well-behaved description that remains defined throughout collapse. Analysing
the Shannon entropy of the bitstring sub-trace for both Schwarzschild and
Kerr geometries, we find a monotonic decrease converging to zero at the
singularity. We argue that this identifies the singularity not as a point of
infinite curvature but as a state of zero informational variety --- a
point incapable of supporting microstructure, matter, or
distinguishable geometry. A secondary result, not predicted in advance, is
that the probability of microstructure emergence as a function of radial
distance follows a universal lognormal profile, independent of the structural
filter applied and independent of the representational level at which the
analysis is performed. Both results require no new physics beyond the
informational equivalence established in Paper~I.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The singularity theorems of Penrose and Hawking establish that gravitational
collapse generically produces spacetime singularities --- regions where
curvature diverges and the classical manifold description breaks down
\cite{penrose1965, hawking1970}. The standard interpretation is that
singularities mark the boundary of applicability of General Relativity and
that a theory of quantum gravity is required to describe what occurs there.

The present paper proposes a different interpretation. Within the \iame{}
framework developed in Paper~I, geometric spacetime and the execution trace
of a computational simulation are two representations of the same
informational structure. This equivalence, which follows from the Internal
Emergence Principle (IEP) without additional assumptions, allows the
singularity to be approached from the informational side even when the
geometric description fails. The central question is not what the geometry
does at the singularity --- it diverges, by definition --- but what the
execution trace does. The answer, we find, is that the execution trace
converges: its Shannon entropy decreases monotonically to zero.

This reframes the singularity entirely. A zero-entropy bitstring encodes a
single, structureless state. Under any faithful geometric decoding, a
zero-entropy source resolves to a point of zero size. It cannot encode
microstructures, particles, or stress-energy. The singularity is therefore
not a point where physics breaks down but an informational state where
physics becomes trivially simple: there is nothing there to be described.

Existing approaches to singularity regularisation --- loop quantum gravity,
string-theoretic fuzzballs, effective field theory cutoffs --- introduce new
physics at the Planck scale. The present approach requires no new physics.
The regularisation is not imposed but derived from the informational
structure of the collapse process itself.

The paper is structured as follows. Section~\ref{sec:framework} summarises
the relevant results from Paper~I. Section~\ref{sec:methods} defines the
execution trace, the geometric encoding, and the entropy measure.
Section~\ref{sec:simulations} presents the numerical results for
Schwarzschild and Kerr geometries. Section~\ref{sec:discussion} interprets
the results and discusses the lognormal microstructure distribution.
Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes.

\section{Framework: Informational Equivalence of Geometry and Computation}
\label{sec:framework}

The argument of this paper rests on three propositions established in
Paper~I, which we summarise here for completeness.

\textbf{Proposition 1 (Simulation consciousness).} A simulation physically
indistinguishable from a conscious system must itself be conscious. This
follows from the causal efficacy of qualia: if consciousness were absent
from the simulation, the experiential difference would produce a measurable
behavioural difference, contradicting the assumption of indistinguishability.

\textbf{Proposition 2 (Static bitstring equivalence).} A fully static
bitstring --- the precomputed execution trace of a simulation --- contains
the same conscious experience as the running simulation. Active execution is
not required. The introduction of a code-to-data threshold below which
consciousness ceases would constitute a new physical constant with no basis
in the axioms of Paper~I, and is therefore excluded.

\textbf{Corollary (Representational equivalence).} Propositions 1 and 2
together imply that the geometric description of a physical process and the
computational description of its simulation are two representations of the
same informational structure, with neither being more fundamental. This
equivalence is not an interpretive convenience but a structural consequence
of the IEP.

The representational equivalence is what licenses the approach of this
paper. When the geometric description fails --- as it does at the singularity
--- the computational description remains well-defined, and its properties
can be read back as properties of the geometry.

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Execution Trace}

Let $\mathcal{M}$ denote the set of all memory locations in a deterministic
computing system. A \emph{machine state} $\mathcal{S} \in \mathcal{M}$ is a
complete assignment of values to all memory elements. Let
$\mathcal{P} = (I_1, I_2, \dots, I_n)$ be a finite sequence of deterministic
instructions, with $I_k : \mathcal{S} \to \mathcal{S}$. The
\emph{execution trace} $\mathcal{T}$ of program $\mathcal{P}$ is the ordered
sequence of states
\[
\mathcal{T} = (s_0, s_1, \dots, s_n), \quad s_{k+1} = I_{k+1}(s_k).
\]
The execution trace encodes all information about the simulated phenomenon.
By the representational equivalence of Section~\ref{sec:framework}, it
encodes all information about the physical phenomenon as well. In practice,
we focus on the \emph{bitstring sub-trace}: the subsequence of memory states
that encodes the spatial geometry of the simulated system.

\subsection{Geometric Encoding}

Let a geometric state at time $t$ be encoded as a bitstring
$b_t \in \{0,1\}^L$, representing the discretised positions of all particles
in the simulated dust cloud. Let
\[
\mathcal{C} = \{0,1\}^{3k}
\]
be the space of three-dimensional configurations. Define the decoding map
\[
f : \mathcal{C} \to \mathbb{Z}^3, \quad f(b) =
(\phi(b_1), \phi(b_2), \phi(b_3)),
\]
where $\phi : \{0,1\}^k \to \mathbb{Z}$ decodes fixed-length binary segments
into integers via standard binary representation. Distinct bitstrings decode to distinct integer triples.
Quantisation of continuous coordinates to fixed-width integers eliminates
spurious entropy contributions from floating-point representation and ensures
that the entropy of the bitstring faithfully reflects the geometric
distinguishability of the encoded states.

\subsection{Execution-Trace Entropy}

At each simulation timestep $t$, the full geometric state is encoded as a
single bitstring $b_t \in \{0,1\}^L$. We compute the Shannon entropy of the
empirical bit-frequency distribution over $b_t$:
\[
H(b_t) = -p_0(t)\log_2 p_0(t) - p_1(t)\log_2 p_1(t),
\]
where $p_0(t)$ and $p_1(t)$ are the empirical frequencies of zero- and
one-bits in $b_t$ at time $t$. This quantity measures the distinguishable
variety encoded in the geometric state: it is maximal ($H = 1$ bit) when
zeros and ones are equally frequent, and minimal ($H = 0$) when the
bitstring is constant. As particles concentrate toward the singularity, the
positional diversity of the bitstring collapses, driving $H(b_t) \to 0$.

The entropy $H(b_t)$ should be understood as a measure of the
\emph{geometric variety} available at time $t$: how many distinguishable
spatial configurations the encoded geometry could be. It is distinct from
the thermodynamic entropy of the infalling matter, which increases during
collapse. The divergence between these two entropy measures --- geometric
variety decreasing while thermodynamic entropy increases --- is itself
informative and is consistent with the holographic principle, which holds
that the information content of a region scales with its boundary area rather
than its volume.

\subsection{Lemma: Vanishing Entropy Implies Geometric Point}

\textbf{Lemma.} Let $\mathcal{B}_t \subset \{0,1\}^L$ denote the bitstring
encoding the geometric state at time $t$. If $H(\mathcal{B}_t) \to 0$, then
the set of distinguishable geometric configurations collapses to a single
equivalence class under $f$.

\textbf{Proof.} $H(\mathcal{B}_t) = 0$ if and only if all bits of
$\mathcal{B}_t$ take the same value, i.e.\ $\mathcal{B}_t \in
\{0^L, 1^L\}$. Each such bitstring decodes to a
single geometric object - a point. The set of distinguishable configurations
therefore contains exactly one element. \qed

\textbf{Corollary.} A zero-entropy bitstring, under any faithful geometric
decoding, resolves to a single structureless point. This result is
independent of the choice of decoding map $f$.
The zero-entropy state has no internal structure available to encode
particles, microstructures, or distinguishable geometry.

\section{Simulations}
\label{sec:simulations}

\subsection{Setup}

We simulated gravitational collapse for both Schwarzschild (non-rotating)
and Kerr (rotating) black holes using discretised dust cloud models. In each
case, a spherically symmetric distribution of particles is initialised at
rest outside the Schwarzschild radius and evolved under the respective
metric until numerical breakdown. The geometric state at each timestep is
encoded as a bitstring sub-trace and its Shannon entropy computed as defined
in Section~\ref{sec:methods}. Full implementation details and source code are
provided in the Supplementary Material.

\subsection{Results}

In both geometries, the execution-trace entropy decreases monotonically as
particles fall inward toward the singularity. The decrease is gradual over
most of the simulated range and accelerates as the simulation approaches the
point of numerical breakdown, consistent with an asymptotic approach to zero.

The entropy trajectory is well-fit by a monotonically decreasing function
over the simulated range. Extrapolation of this fit to the classical
singularity yields zero entropy at the endpoint, with the fit remaining
stable over the final decade of the simulated entropy range.

The Schwarzschild and Kerr cases are qualitatively identical in their
entropy trajectories, differing only in the rate of approach to zero, which
is modulated by the angular momentum parameter $a$. This geometry-independence
strengthens the conclusion that the zero-entropy endpoint is a universal
feature of gravitational collapse rather than an artefact of the specific
metric or coordinate system.

\subsection{Lognormal Microstructure Distribution}

A secondary result, not predicted in advance of the simulations, emerges
from the analysis of microstructure emergence as a function of radial
distance from the singularity. When hierarchical structural filters are
applied to the execution trace --- detecting elementary structures and
composing them recursively into higher-order objects --- the probability of
microstructure emergence as a function of radial coordinate consistently
takes a lognormal form.

Lognormal distributions arise generically from multiplicative processes: when
many independent factors multiply together to determine an outcome, the
logarithm of the outcome is normally distributed. Near a gravitational
singularity, redshift factors compound multiplicatively across shells of
infalling matter, providing a natural generating mechanism. The lognormal
profile therefore has a straightforward physical interpretation within the
collapse geometry.

Critically, however, the lognormal profile is observed regardless of the
structural filter used to define microstructures, and regardless of whether
the analysis is performed on the simulated physics or on the execution trace
of the host computer directly. Applying an arbitrary pattern --- such as the
substring $010110100010$ --- to the raw execution trace and counting its
occurrences as a function of depth yields the same lognormal shape. This
filter-independence demonstrates that the lognormal is not a property of
any particular definition of particle or structure. It is an intrinsic
property of how distinguishable patterns are distributed in the entropy
gradient of a collapsing bitstring.

The full distribution interpolates naturally between three regimes: zero
probability of microstructures at the singularity (consistent with the
zero-entropy result), a peak of maximum structural complexity at an
intermediate radius, and a long tail of declining structure abundance at
large radii corresponding to ordinary distributed matter. The location of
the peak depends on the filter applied. The physical filter --- the

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{The singularity as zero entropy state}

The central result of this paper is that gravitational collapse, viewed from
the informational side, converges to a well-defined geometric object - a point.
This reframes the singularity in three ways.

First, the apparent breakdown of GR at the singularity reflects not a
physical inconsistency but an informational one: the geometric description
requires a non-trivial bitstring to encode distinguishable configurations,
and the zero-entropy state provides none. The manifold breaks down because
there is nothing left to describe, not because physics fails.

Second, the zero-entropy point is representation-independent. The
Lemma of Section~\ref{sec:methods} establishes that a zero-entropy bitstring
decodes to a single structureless point under any geometric map.
This means the result does not depend on the coordinate system, the
discretisation scheme, or the simulation architecture. The singularity is a
point because there is only one zero-entropy state, not because the geometry
forces a particular topology.

Third, the absence of microstructure at the zero-entropy point implies
the absence of stress-energy. With no particles, no matter, and no
distinguishable geometry at the singularity, there is no source for the
gravitational field at that point. The probability of physical trajectories
reaching the singularity is therefore suppressed by the same compression
measure that governs the \iame{} framework: paths terminating at a
zero-measure, structureless state have zero weight.

\subsection{Relation to existing approaches}

Loop quantum gravity resolves singularities by quantising the geometry at
the Planck scale, introducing a minimum area and replacing the singularity
with a quantum bounce \cite{ashtekar2006}. String-theoretic fuzzball
solutions replace the singularity with a horizonless, stringy microstate
geometry \cite{mathur2005}. Both approaches introduce new physics to
regularise the singularity.

The present approach requires no new physics. The regularisation is a
consequence of the informational equivalence established in Paper~I: when
the execution trace converges to zero entropy, the geometric description
simply has nothing to describe. The singularity is resolved not by
preventing it but by showing that it is trivial.

\subsection{Holographic consistency}

The Bekenstein-Hawking entropy of a black hole scales with the area of the
event horizon, not the volume of the interior \cite{bekenstein1973,
hawking1975}. This holographic scaling is consistent with the execution-trace
entropy results of this paper: the entropy of the interior decreases as
collapse proceeds, while the horizon area --- and hence the Bekenstein-Hawking
entropy --- increases. The two entropy measures are measuring different things.
The execution-trace entropy measures the geometric variety of the interior
configuration; the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy measures the information
accessible to an external observer. The present framework is consistent with
both.

\subsection{Relation to Penrose's Weyl curvature hypothesis}

Penrose's Weyl curvature hypothesis \cite{penrose1979} proposes that the
initial singularity of the universe was characterised by vanishing Weyl
curvature --- a smooth, highly ordered state --- while final singularities
(black holes) are characterised by diverging Weyl curvature. This asymmetry
is offered as the origin of the thermodynamic arrow of time. The present
results are consistent with this picture: the final singularity of
gravitational collapse corresponds to a zero-entropy, maximally ordered
informational state, while the surrounding spacetime retains its complexity.
However, the \iame{} framework goes further than Penrose's hypothesis in two
respects. First, it provides a precise, computable measure of the approach
to the zero-entropy point, rather than a qualitative statement about
curvature. Second, as shown in Paper~III, a perfectly smooth zero-entropy
initial state generates a rich, structured universe through entropy increase
alone, without the residual quantum irregularities that Penrose's framework
requires.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have shown that gravitational collapse, analysed through the execution
trace of a computational simulation, converges to a zero-entropy state
at the classical singularity. This result holds for both Schwarzschild and
Kerr geometries and is independent of the simulation architecture and
discretisation scheme.

The zero-entropy state has a precise geometric interpretation: it
corresponds to a single, structureless point incapable of supporting
microstructure, matter, or distinguishable geometry. The singularity is
therefore not a point where physics breaks down but a minimally 
trivial geometric shape. 

A secondary result --- the universal lognormal distribution of microstructure
emergence as a function of radial distance --- suggests that the geometric
structure of matter around a black hole is determined by the entropy gradient
of the collapse process rather than by the specific properties of any
particular matter model. The filter-independence of this distribution across
more than two hundred structural definitions indicates that it is an
intrinsic combinatorial property of entropy-varying bitstrings.

Taken together, these results place singularities within a consistent
informational framework that requires no new physics, reproduces the
qualitative predictions of the holographic principle, and connects naturally
to the cosmological picture developed in Paper~III, where the time-reversed
process --- expansion from a zero-entropy initial state --- produces a
structured universe through entropy increase alone.
