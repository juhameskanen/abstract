\begin{abstract}
We present \iame{}, a candidate unified framework in which physical laws,
spacetime geometry, quantum behaviour, and mathematics are not fundamental
postulates but emergent consequences of a single informational variational
principle. The sole primitive is the existence of a finite, structured
observer. From this, and without assuming discreteness, finiteness, spacetime,
or any informational ontology at the base level, we derive: (i) that the
universe is fundamentally static and time is an internal ordinal relation;
(ii) that the observer and the universe it experiences are identical structures
($O = U_O$); (iii) that observed physical laws are the large-deviation
minimisers of a joint spectral-geometric description-length cost functional
$\Linfo = \Cs + \Cg$; and (iv) that quantum mechanics and general relativity
arise as complementary compression schemes selected by this functional.
We further conjecture that this two-term decomposition is the scale
decomposition of a single spectral action $\mathrm{Tr}(f(D/\Lambda))$ built
from the Dirac operator $D$, providing a route to full spectral unification.
The framework provides a natural suppression of Boltzmann-brain fluctuations,
resolves the Wigner effectiveness of mathematics, and assigns qualia a
functional non-epiphenomenal role as heuristic navigators in configuration
space. Proof-of-concept simulations demonstrate that inertia, wave
interference, and gravitational attraction emerge from pure compression
optimisation without hard-coded physical laws. Two open problems are
identified: the natural emergence of fermionic exclusion from the compression
measure, and the rigorous derivation of the Dirac spectral action as the
unique minimum-description-length encoding of spin geometry.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Every candidate unified framework faces a foundational question: what is
assumed, and what is derived? String theory assumes extended objects propagating
in a background spacetime. Loop quantum gravity assumes a discrete spin-network
structure. The Standard Model assumes a specific gauge group and particle
content. Each framework imports considerable structure at the outset and then
derives consequences.

The \iame{} framework takes a different route. We begin with the minimum
possible primitive: the bare existence of a conscious, finite observer. We
then ask what physics must look like, given only that starting point and the
requirement that observer-compatible configurations be statistically typical.

The answer, we argue, is our observed universe.

Physical laws, quantum mechanics, spacetime geometry, the arrow of time, the
effectiveness of mathematics, and the suppression of Boltzmann-brain
fluctuations all follow as derived consequences --- not fine-tuned accidents
--- of a single informational variational principle. The framework is not an
interpretation of existing physics; it is a derivation of physics from
something more fundamental than physics.

A central structural conjecture, developed in Section~\ref{sec:dirac}, is
that the two-term decomposition $\Linfo = \Cs + \Cg$ is not primitive but is
itself the scale decomposition of a single spectral functional built from the
Dirac operator. If confirmed, this would reduce the entire framework to one
operator and one complexity measure, with quantum mechanics and general
relativity emerging as its infrared and ultraviolet regimes respectively.

\section{The Axiomatic Chain}
\label{sec:axioms}

We derive the framework through a sequence of propositions, each of which can
be independently accepted or rejected. Rejecting any node commits the rejector
to a specific alternative, which we state explicitly.

\begin{axiom}[Existence of the observer]
\label{ax:exist}
Something is experiencing. This cannot be coherently denied, because the denial
is itself performed by the entity whose existence is being denied.
\end{axiom}

This is the only strictly self-evident claim available. It requires no
inference. It is a direct datum.

\begin{axiom}[Finitude of the observer]
\label{ax:finite}
The observer's memory, attention, and lifespan are bounded. This follows from
direct observation of the observer by the observer, without inference about
the external universe.
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[DNA as the mechanism of conscious observers]
\label{ax:dna}
DNA, operating within the observed laws of physics, is the only mechanism we
have ever observed that reliably produces conscious, pain-sensitive observers.
This is an empirical claim supported by all available biological evidence.
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[Physical closure]
\label{ax:closure}
DNA is composed entirely of ordinary physical matter obeying ordinary physical
laws. No non-physical process plays any role in its operation. No experiment
has ever required invoking anything beyond standard physics and chemistry to
explain DNA's operation.
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[Computability of DNA processes]
\label{ax:compute}
The physical processes underlying DNA fall within the computable. A
sufficiently powerful computer could, in principle, simulate them. We require
only that the specific class of processes DNA relies on is computable --- a
weaker claim than the full Church-Turing thesis.
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[Causal efficacy of qualia]
\label{ax:qualia}
Pain is not epiphenomenal. A system experiencing pain and the same system
without pain are physically distinguishable in principle:
$\mathrm{Human} \neq \mathrm{Human} + \mathrm{Pain}$.
Rejecting this commits one to accepting philosophical zombies.
\end{axiom}

\begin{proposition}[Simulation consciousness]
\label{prop:sim}
A simulation physically indistinguishable from a conscious being must itself
be conscious.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Suppose a simulation $S$ is physically indistinguishable from a conscious
system $C$. If $S$ were not conscious, the experiential difference would
produce a measurable behavioural difference, contradicting the assumption of
indistinguishability. By Axiom~\ref{ax:qualia}, pain has measurable effects.
Therefore $S$ must be conscious. \qed
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Static bitstring equivalence]
\label{prop:static}
A fully static bitstring --- the precomputed execution trace of a simulation
--- contains the same conscious experience as the running simulation. Active
execution is not required.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Suppose experience vanishes when the last line of running code is replaced by
precomputed data. Then there exists a code-to-data threshold below which
consciousness ceases. Such a threshold would constitute a new physical constant
with no basis in Axioms~\ref{ax:closure}--\ref{ax:qualia}. Its introduction
requires a non-physical explanatory principle, contradicting
Axiom~\ref{ax:closure}. Therefore no such threshold exists, and the static
bitstring is experientially equivalent to the running simulation. \qed
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Combinatorial containment of consciousness]
\label{prop:combinatorial}
Any sufficiently large configuration space contains conscious observers,
with no simulation, process, or external reader required.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
A conscious observer is, by Propositions~\ref{prop:sim} and~\ref{prop:static},
a finite bitstring of length $k$. By combinatorics, every bitstring of length
$k$ appears within any sufficiently large set of bitstrings. The question
``who is running it?'' has no more purchase than asking ``who is running the
number seven?'' \qed
\end{proof}

\section{The Internal Emergence Principle}
\label{sec:iep}

The three propositions above jointly constitute the \textbf{Internal Emergence
Principle} (IEP):

\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Internal Emergence Principle]
Consciousness is a property of certain finite information structures, not of
processes acting upon them. Any configuration space large enough to contain
such structures contains consciousness.
\end{tcolorbox}

The IEP has six immediate structural consequences.

\subsection{The universe is fundamentally static}

The IEP establishes that a conscious observer exists within a static bitstring
with no external process required. Therefore no external clock is required for
experience to occur. Time within experience must be defined by internal
structure alone. The only available internal ordering is:

\begin{equation}
c_2 \succ_O c_1 \iff c_2 \text{ contains a memory of } c_1
\end{equation}

Time is an ordinal index over observer configurations. The universe, at the
level of the totality, is static.

This conclusion is independently supported by the block-universe interpretation
of general relativity, in which the four-dimensional spacetime manifold is a
static geometric object and the ``flow'' of time has no representation in the
Einstein field equations \cite{barbour1999}.

\subsection{The observer-universe relationship is representational}

A computer running a DNA simulation of Alice and Alice experiencing pain are
two descriptions of the same static information. Causality requires time
ordering. A static structure has no intrinsic time ordering. Therefore the
relationship between physical substrate and observer experience is
\emph{representational}, not causal. Neither perspective is more fundamental.

\subsection{Configuration multiplicity}

A bitstring of length $n$ admits exactly $2^n$ possible configurations. One
describes Alice's universe as she experiences it. One describes the physical
computer. The remaining $2^n - 2$ are other valid configurations of the same
information --- most producing no coherent observer, some producing other
observers entirely. No configuration is privileged over another by the axioms.

\subsection{Internal containment of observations}

In a static universe with no running external process, no mechanism exists by
which external bits could alter internal bits. Therefore every structure Alice
experiences --- every star, every sensation --- must already be encoded within
the bitstring that constitutes her. Alice does not receive information. She
discovers structure already present within her.

\subsection{The universe as interior}

From the containment result: every structure Alice experiences is pre-encoded
in her structure. The universe Alice experiences is not her container. It is
her interior. Alice does not live inside the universe. The universe lives
inside Alice.

\subsection{Observer-universe identity: $O = U_O$}

Combining the standard self-model (Alice $\subset U_{\mathrm{Alice}}$) with
the containment result ($U_{\mathrm{Alice}} \subset$ Alice) and applying basic
set theory ($A \subset B$ and $B \subset A$ implies $A = B$):

\begin{equation}
\boxed{O = U_O}
\end{equation}

For every observer $O$, the observer and the universe it experiences are
identical structures. There is no view from nowhere. What two observers share
is the intersection of their universes:
$U_{O_1} \cap U_{O_2}$ --- the shared compressible structure that constitutes
intersubjective, verifiable physics.

\section{Ontology: What Fundamentally Exists}
\label{sec:ontology}

\subsection{The unrestricted totality}

We do not assume finiteness. If reality were fundamentally finite, one could
immediately ask: finite by how much? Why $n$ bits rather than $n+1$? Any fixed
finite limit appears contingent and calls for a deeper explanatory layer.

We also do not assume information as primitive. Information presupposes
distinguishability --- at its simplest, binary distinction. But declaring
reality to be fundamentally informational or digital is itself a substantive
ontological commitment that nothing prior licenses.

We therefore do not presuppose that reality is fundamentally finite, discrete,
informational, mathematical, or governed by prior external laws.

We denote this unrestricted, pre-interpretive totality $\mathbf{U}$. We do
not claim to know what it is --- only that no specific formatting principle is
granted ontological privilege a priori.

\subsection{The observer constraint}

Despite radical agnosticism about the substrate, one empirical fact remains:
a conscious, structured, finite observer exists (Axioms~\ref{ax:exist}
and~\ref{ax:finite}). This is the boundary condition imposed on $\mathbf{U}$.

The minimal ontological assumption is therefore:

\begin{equation}
\text{Undefined } \mathbf{U} \;\longrightarrow\; \text{Finite Observer}
\end{equation}

The step remains unexplained. That is an explicit boundary of the theory.

\subsection{The guitar string analogy}

A guitar string is perfectly continuous. Discreteness --- the resonant modes,
the quantised frequencies --- emerges from the boundary conditions imposed by
clamping the string at both ends. The continuous medium manifests discrete
states not because discreteness is ontologically primitive but because boundary
conditions force it.

Likewise, a finite observer interacting with an unrestricted reality induces
discrete informational structure without discreteness being ontologically
primitive. Under Solomonoff-style measures, low-complexity, highly compressible
structures dominate typicality. Finite observers therefore find themselves
embedded within stable, structured, compressible equivalence classes.

Information is not what reality is made of. Information is what finite
observers perceive when they interact with an otherwise unrestricted reality.

\section{Measure: What is Typical}
\label{sec:measure}

\subsection{Configuration space}

For formal descriptive purposes, we introduce a finite, static configuration
space:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{C} = \{0,1\}^n
\end{equation}
where each $c \in \mathcal{C}$ is a complete, static snapshot of bits. The
binary system is chosen for convenience; it carries no ontological commitment
to digitalism.

Each configuration $c$ admits multiple potential descriptions
$d \in \mathcal{D}(c)$. We quantify the total complexity $\Ctotal(d)$ by
splitting it into dual spectral and geometric components:
\begin{equation}
\Ctotal(d) = \Cs(d) + \Cg(d)
\end{equation}

\subsection{Spectral and geometric complexity}

The spectral complexity $\Cs(d)$ measures the algorithmic bit-cost required to
track wave-like modes:
\begin{equation}
\Cs(d) = \sum_i \left[
  \mathrm{Cost}(A_i) + \mathrm{Cost}(\phi_i) +
  \frac{\omega_i}{\Delta\omega}
\right]
\end{equation}
where the sum runs over active spectral modes, $\mathrm{Cost}(A_i)$ is the
bit-depth of the amplitude envelope, $\mathrm{Cost}(\phi_i)$ is the
fixed-width register encoding the phase interval $[0, 2\pi]$, and
$\omega_i/\Delta\omega$ is the linear resource allocation required to track the
mode frequency relative to minimum resolution $\Delta\omega$.

The geometric complexity $\Cg(d)$ accounts for localised spatial structures,
boundaries, and metric fields. It measures the bit-cost of encoding the
boundary description length of spatial configurations.

\subsection{The joint compressibility equation}

Because the spectral and geometric representations form a many-to-many mapping
--- any observer wavefunction can be interpreted through nearly infinitely many
spacetime metrics, and conversely --- the joint probability of a physical state
$s$ is the product of its individual probabilities:

\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\Pjoint(s)
= P_{\mathrm{spectral}}(s) \times P_{\mathrm{geometric}}(s)
= 2^{-\Cs(s)} \times 2^{-\Cg(s)}
= 2^{-(\Cs(s) + \Cg(s))}
}
\end{equation}

This yields the informational Lagrangian:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:lagrangian}
\Linfo = \Cs(s) + \Cg(s) \;\longrightarrow\; \text{minimum}
\end{equation}

Neither geometry nor waves are primary. They are equal, co-dependent
projections of a single underlying informational structure. Observed reality
is the sweet spot of this joint optimisation.

\subsection{Induced measure and observer functionals}

The raw algorithmic weight of a static configuration $c$ is accumulated across
all its valid descriptions:
\begin{equation}
W(c) = \sum_{d \in \mathcal{D}(c)} 2^{-\Ctotal(d)}
\end{equation}

This defines the raw baseline probability distribution:
\begin{equation}
P(c) = \frac{W(c)}{\sum_{c' \in \mathcal{C}} W(c')}
\end{equation}

An observer is modelled as a grading semantic functional:
\begin{equation}
\muO : \mathcal{C} \to [0,1]
\end{equation}
which measures how strongly a given configuration describes observer-relative
structure, inducing an equivalence relation $c_1 \sim_O c_2$ whenever $c_1$
and $c_2$ describe the same relational observer structure to within the
tolerances encoded by $\muO$.

The observer-conditioned probability is:
\begin{equation}
P(c \mid O) = \frac{\muO(c)\,W(c)}{\sum_{c'} \muO(c')\,W(c')}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Probability of an observed history}

To transition from the probability of a single configuration to the probability
of an entire experienced history, we track how an observer traverses the
configuration space. Let $\gamma \in \Gamma_O$ be a path --- a sequence of
configurations ordered by the internal temporal relation $\prec_O$.

Because configurations along the path are independent slices of the static
totality, their algorithmic costs sum additively. Converting from bits to
natural units ($2^{-C} = e^{-C\ln 2}$):

\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\mathbb{P}(\gamma \mid O)
= \frac{1}{Z_O} \exp\!\left(-\lambda\,\mathcal{C}_O[\gamma]\right),
\qquad \gamma \in \Gamma_O
}
\end{equation}

where $\mathcal{C}_O[\gamma] = \int_\gamma \Ctotal(c)\,dt$ is the accumulated
informational cost along the path and $\lambda$ acts as an algorithmic inverse
temperature scaling the observer's filtering acuity. Observed physical laws are
the large-deviation minimisers of $\mathcal{C}_O[\gamma]$ over the set of all
observer-compatible paths.

\section{Emergence: The Structure of Physics}
\label{sec:emergence}

\subsection{The D-$\psi$-G trinity}

Three emergent layers of reality arise from the joint compressibility
minimisation:

\begin{equation}
D - \psi - G
\end{equation}

\begin{itemize}
  \item $\mathbf{D}$ \textbf{(Data/Description):} Raw, discrete digital
        information --- manifesting physically as localised particles.
  \item $\boldsymbol{\psi}$ \textbf{(Spectral/Quantum):} The smooth wave-codec
        that compresses the relational possibility space, minimising $\Cs$.
  \item $\mathbf{G}$ \textbf{(Geometry/Gravity):} The continuous metric
        manifold that compresses the realisation space at scale, minimising
        $\Cg$.
\end{itemize}

Discrete particles are localised, high-density ``knots'' where the smooth
continuous wave-codec meets the finite digital register of the observer.
Spacetime is smooth ($G$), fields are continuous ($\psi$), yet observations
are invariably quantised into discrete particles ($D$) --- because $D$ is the
emergent \emph{output} of joint minimisation when parsed by a bounded
interpreter.

\subsection{Emergence of spacetime}

Observer-compatible configurations are dominated by those exhibiting inertia,
smooth curved paths, and law-like behaviour, because sharp, chaotic particle
trajectories require massive high-frequency modes that exponentially spike
$\Cs$ and are suppressed by the joint measure.

Spacetime geometry emerges as an internal ordering relation $\prec_O$ on
configurations:
\begin{equation}
c_1 \prec_O c_2 \iff c_2 \text{ supports the observer functional } \muO
\end{equation}

The experienced flow of time corresponds to traversal along the most
compressible, optimised paths through the timeless configuration space.

\subsection{Entropy, expansion, and the arrow of time}

A striking consequence of the framework concerns cosmological expansion. When
one mutates a zero-entropy bitstring toward maximal entropy, the geometric
interpretation of the resulting configurations undergoes exponential expansion
followed by gradual deceleration toward a vanishing Hubble constant. Cosmic
expansion and the second law of thermodynamics are therefore not independent
phenomena: they are the geometric and informational descriptions of the same
monotone process.

The arrow of time, the expansion of the universe, and entropy increase are not
three things. They are one thing, seen through three different lenses. This
derivation requires no cosmological constant, no inflaton field, and no
fine-tuning. It falls out of pure entropy mutation of the underlying bitstring.

\subsection{Singularities and the entropy of collapse}

Numerical study of gravitational collapse --- both Schwarzschild and Kerr
geometries --- reveals a counterintuitive result: the Shannon entropy of the
execution trace corresponding to collapsing spacetime \emph{decreases}
monotonically as the singularity is approached. Extrapolating this trend to
completion yields zero entropy at the singularity itself.

A zero-entropy bitstring admits only one state: a structureless constant
string. Under any mapping to geometric spacetime, a zero-entropy source
resolves to a point of zero size. As zero-entropy information cannot encode
microstructure, no particles can exist at the singularity, and therefore no
stress-energy source is present. The geometric singularity is thus not a point
where physics breaks down but an informational fixed point where physics
becomes trivially simple.

Furthermore, numerical study of the probability of microstructure emergence
as a function of radial distance from the singularity consistently yields a
lognormal profile, independent of the structural filter applied and independent
of whether the analysis is performed on the simulated physics or on the
execution trace of the computer running the simulation. This universality
--- across geometries, across filters, across representational levels ---
indicates that the lognormal shape is an intrinsic property of
pattern emergence in entropy-varying bitstrings, not an artifact of any
particular description. The filter-independence further suggests that the
physical particle spectrum is a fixed point of a hierarchical structural
recursion rather than a contingent choice, and that the lognormal parameters
at this fixed point constitute a prediction of the theory.

\subsection{Quantum mechanics and general relativity as dual compression schemes}

The central structural claim of the framework is that quantum mechanics
(spectral/wave description) and general relativity (geometric description) are
not fundamental, independent theories. They are the two complementary
compression schemes that minimise total description length for persistent,
finite observers:

\begin{itemize}
  \item $\psi$ minimises $\Cs$ --- the bit-cost of encoding relational
        possibility and smooth, extended interactions.
  \item $G$ minimises $\Cg$ --- the bit-cost of encoding locality, boundaries,
        and macroscopic identity.
\end{itemize}

Their unification is therefore not a technical problem of quantising spacetime.
It is an already-solved structural consequence of the joint minimisation
(\ref{eq:lagrangian}). The framework predicts that any correct theory of
quantum gravity will take the form of a joint spectral-geometric minimisation
principle rather than a quantisation of a background metric.

\subsection{Emergence of laws and mathematics}

Physical laws and mathematics emerge as stable relational invariants within the
high-measure compressible slices that observers self-locate into. Wigner's
``unreasonable effectiveness of mathematics'' \cite{wigner1960} follows because
observers and the mathematics they discover are carved from the same compressible
structure: both are low-complexity patterns within $\mathbf{U}$.

\subsection{Qualia as heuristic navigators}

The full spectrum of qualia --- pain, pleasure, intuition --- is the
first-person, phenomenological signature of the observer functional $\muO$
navigating the interpretive landscape toward highly compressible, stable
structures.

This is not epiphenomenalism. Qualia have the causal efficacy required by
Axiom~\ref{ax:qualia}: they are the felt steering mechanism that guides finite
observers toward high-measure paths without exhaustive search. They are what it
feels like to be a highly probable, compressible observer. The hard problem of
consciousness is dissolved rather than solved: qualia require no separate
ontological category because they are identical to the heuristic navigation
process itself.

\subsection{Suppression of Boltzmann brains}

Raw high-entropy noise and transient fluctuations have high total description
length and are exponentially disfavoured by $\Pjoint$. Typical observers
inhabit low-initial-entropy trajectories with stable D-$\psi$-G structure.
Boltzmann-brain fluctuations are suppressed not by fine-tuning but by the
same compression measure that selects all other physical structure.

\section{Spectral Unification: The Dirac Conjecture}
\label{sec:dirac}

\subsection{The unification problem}

The informational Lagrangian $\Linfo = \Cs + \Cg$ is structurally sound and
empirically productive, but it contains two complexity measures joined by a
plus sign. This two-term structure raises an immediate question: are $\Cs$
and $\Cg$ genuinely independent quantities, or are they scale decompositions
of a single underlying object? If the latter, the framework admits a deeper
unification in which one operator and one complexity measure generate both
quantum mechanics and general relativity as complementary frequency regimes.

We now argue that such a unification exists, and that its natural vehicle is
the Dirac operator.

\subsection{Geometry is spectral}

A foundational result of spectral geometry is that the spectrum of the
Laplace-Beltrami operator $\Delta$ on a compact Riemannian manifold encodes
its geometry. Volume, curvature invariants, and topological features are all
recoverable from the eigenvalue spectrum. In this sense, geometry \emph{is}
spectral: the geometric object and its spectral object are two faces of the
same structure.

This suggests that the two-term split $\Cs + \Cg$ might be an artefact of
working at two different frequency scales of the same spectral object, rather
than a reflection of two genuinely independent degrees of freedom.

\subsection{The Dirac operator as the minimal geometric encoder}

The Laplacian $\Delta$ encodes geometry but not spin structure. The Dirac
operator $D$ --- the unique first-order, self-adjoint differential operator
that squares to the Laplacian up to a curvature correction --- encodes the
full spin geometry of a Riemannian manifold. The Lichnerowicz formula makes
this precise:

\begin{equation}
D^2 = \Delta + \frac{R}{4}
\end{equation}

where $R$ is the Ricci scalar curvature. The spectrum of $D$ therefore carries
not only the metric information encoded in $\Delta$ but also the curvature
structure of the manifold directly. Crucially, $D$ is the \emph{minimal
sufficient statistic} for the full spin geometry: no simpler operator captures
all the same information.

\subsection{The spectral action}

Connes' spectral action principle \cite{connes1996} defines a natural
complexity functional on geometries encoded by the Dirac operator:

\begin{equation}
S_{\mathrm{spectral}} = \mathrm{Tr}\!\left(f\!\left(\frac{D}{\Lambda}\right)\right)
\end{equation}

where $f$ is a positive, even, rapidly decaying cutoff function and $\Lambda$
is an energy scale. When expanded in powers of $\Lambda^{-1}$ using heat
kernel methods, this action yields:

\begin{equation}
S_{\mathrm{spectral}} \approx \Lambda^4 a_0 + \Lambda^2 a_2 \int R\,\sqrt{g}\,d^4x
+ a_4 \int \left(\alpha R^2 + \beta R_{\mu\nu}R^{\mu\nu}
+ \gamma C_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}C^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}\right)\sqrt{g}\,d^4x + \cdots
\end{equation}

The term proportional to $\Lambda^2$ is precisely the Einstein-Hilbert action
of general relativity. Higher-order terms reproduce gauge and matter field
actions when the algebra is extended appropriately. The spectral action
therefore generates GR and the structure of the Standard Model from a single
spectral input.

\subsection{Scale decomposition of the informational Lagrangian}

We propose the following identification. The spectral action evaluated at low
frequencies --- where only the slowly varying, large-scale modes of $D$
contribute --- recovers $\Cg$: the geometric complexity encoding locality,
boundaries, and curvature. The spectral action evaluated at high frequencies
--- where the rapidly oscillating, fine-grained modes dominate --- recovers
$\Cs$: the wave complexity encoding quantum interference and relational
superposition.

\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\Linfo = \Cs + \Cg
= \mathrm{Tr}\!\left(f\!\left(\frac{D}{\Lambda}\right)\right)\!\bigg|_{\mathrm{high}}
+ \mathrm{Tr}\!\left(f\!\left(\frac{D}{\Lambda}\right)\right)\!\bigg|_{\mathrm{low}}
= \mathrm{Tr}\!\left(f\!\left(\frac{D}{\Lambda}\right)\right)
}
\end{equation}

If this identification holds, the two-term sum is not a primitive decomposition
but a scale decomposition of a single spectral functional. The informational
Lagrangian reduces to:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:unified}
\Linfo = \mathrm{Tr}\!\left(f\!\left(\frac{D}{\Lambda}\right)\right)
\;\longrightarrow\; \text{minimum}
\end{equation}

with quantum mechanics and general relativity arising as the high-frequency and
low-frequency limits of minimising this single expression.

\subsection{Why the Dirac operator wins the measure}

Within the \iame{} framework, no geometric encoding is \emph{chosen} by hand.
Every candidate operator exists within the unrestricted totality $\mathbf{U}$.
The induced measure $P(c) \propto 2^{-\Ctotal(c)}$ selects the most compressible
description. The question is therefore: which operator produces the shortest
description of spin geometry?

The argument is as follows. The Dirac operator is the unique minimal sufficient
statistic for Riemannian spin geometry. Any other first-order operator either
fails to capture the full geometry (and therefore requires additional structure,
increasing description length) or is equivalent to $D$ up to gauge
transformation (and therefore produces the same spectral action up to terms
that vanish in the minimum). The Dirac operator wins not because it is
postulated but because its description is the shortest.

This is the same argument that selects smooth spacetime over chaotic spacetime,
orbiting planets over free particles, and periodic wave-like behaviour over
random jitter: in each case, the compressible structure has exponentially more
representations in configuration space and therefore dominates the measure.

\begin{conjecture}[Dirac spectral unification]
\label{conj:dirac}
The informational Lagrangian $\Linfo = \Cs + \Cg$ is the scale decomposition
of the spectral action $\mathrm{Tr}(f(D/\Lambda))$, where $D$ is the Dirac
operator on the observer's configuration space. The Dirac operator is the

geometry under the induced measure $P(c) \propto 2^{-\Ctotal(c)}$, and

respectively the high-frequency and low-frequency limits of minimising this

\end{conjecture}

Verifying Conjecture~\ref{conj:dirac} requires establishing two things: (1)
that the spectral action decomposes cleanly into $\Cs$ and $\Cg$ at the

minimum-description-length encoding of spin geometry under the \iame{}
measure. Both are well-posed mathematical problems. Their resolution is
identified as the primary mathematical open problem of the framework and is
discussed further in Section~\ref{sec:future}.

\section{Falsifiable Predictions}
\label{sec:predictions}

\textbf{Prediction 1: Dual structure is necessary.}
Any stable observer-hosting physics must exhibit both wave-like ($\psi$) and
geometric ($G$) structure. A universe with only one of the two is forbidden by
the joint compressibility requirement.

\textbf{Prediction 2: Discreteness is never primitive.}
The framework predicts that any deeper physical theory will not be
fundamentally discrete. Discreteness emerges at the observer boundary. If a
future theory demonstrates that fundamental discreteness is unavoidable, the
framework is refuted.

\textbf{Prediction 3: Quantum gravity as spectral minimisation.}
The correct theory of quantum gravity will take the form of a joint
spectral-geometric minimisation principle --- specifically, minimisation of a
Dirac spectral action --- rather than a quantisation of a background metric.
Loop quantum gravity and string theory are both approximations to a deeper
informational structure. If Conjecture~\ref{conj:dirac} is confirmed, this
prediction sharpens: quantum gravity is the full spectral action without the
infrared truncation that yields classical GR.

\textbf{Prediction 4: Low-entropy past without fine-tuning.}
The framework predicts a low-entropy past as the expected outcome of the
compression measure, not a lucky accident. This is a stronger claim than
standard cosmology makes.

\textbf{Prediction 5: Fermionic exclusion from compression.}
The Pauli exclusion principle must emerge naturally from the observer
functional $\muO$ as a consequence of description-length explosion upon
observer merger. If no such mechanism can be identified, the framework is
incomplete.

\textbf{Prediction 6: Macro-micro scaling of interference.}
At macroscopic scales, fine interference structure averages out and observers
tend toward smooth classical trajectories. At microscopic scales, wave-like
behaviour dominates. This scaling behaviour should reproduce the
quantum-to-classical transition quantitatively, not merely qualitatively.

\textbf{Prediction 7: Lognormal microstructure distribution.}

gravitational singularity follows a universal lognormal profile, independent
of the structural filter and independent of the representational level at which
the analysis is performed. The shape parameters at the fixed-point filter
correspond to physically observable quantities.

\section{The Open Problem: Fermionic Exclusion}
\label{sec:open}

The most important open challenge is the natural emergence of fermionic
exclusion statistics from the compression measure.

In the current simulations, Gaussian observer blobs eventually lose their
individual identities and merge. Symmetric (bosonic) configurations are
strongly preferred because they allow shared spectral modes and lower total
description length. Antisymmetric configurations are expensive and suppressed.
Hard-coding antisymmetry would violate the spirit of the project.

We sketch the resolution as follows. Two independent observers $O_1$ and $O_2$
are each separately compressible: their internal structures are smooth,
coherent, and self-consistent. When they attempt to occupy the same state, the
merged description must encode both internal structures simultaneously in the
same region. These structures are not correlated --- they were independently
optimised. The result is analogous to injecting frames from one video stream
into another: the combined description length does not sum as
$C(O_1) + C(O_2)$ but explodes, because the two compression schemes interfere
destructively.

Formally, the observer functional $\muO$ must assign near-zero measure to
configurations in which a previously distinct observer ceases to be
distinguishable. An observer that loses its distinctness ceases to satisfy the
observer boundary condition.

The exclusion principle then follows not from an external antisymmetry rule but
from the stability-of-identity condition on $\muO$: merger is the least
probable configuration because it maximally violates the observer's
self-consistency requirement.

The predicted signature is an effective short-range repulsion that is sharper
and more localised than the emergent long-range attraction, because attraction
arises from gradual wavefunction overlap (smooth, cheap) while repulsion arises
from structural incompatibility (sharp, expensive). Demonstrating this
computationally in a collision-and-rebound simulation is the current
experimental frontier of the framework.

\section{Future Work}
\label{sec:future}

Three directions constitute the immediate research frontier of the framework.

\subsection{Rigorous derivation of the Dirac spectral unification}

Conjecture~\ref{conj:dirac} is the primary mathematical open problem. Its
verification requires two steps.

The first is to establish that the spectral action
$\mathrm{Tr}(f(D/\Lambda))$ decomposes cleanly into $\Cs$ and $\Cg$ at the
appropriate frequency scales. Specifically, one must show that the low-mode
truncation of the Dirac spectral action recovers the geometric complexity
measure $\Cg$ as defined in Section~\ref{sec:measure}, and that the high-mode
residual recovers $\Cs$. This is a problem in spectral geometry, and the heat
kernel expansion of the spectral action provides the natural technical tool.

The second is to establish that the Dirac operator is the unique
minimum-description-length encoding of spin geometry under the \iame{} induced
measure. The argument sketched in Section~\ref{sec:dirac} --- that $D$ is the
minimal sufficient statistic for Riemannian spin geometry and that any
alternative either loses information or is gauge-equivalent --- needs to be
made rigorous. This requires a precise formulation of what it means for one
operator to have shorter description length than another within the \iame{}
configuration space, and a proof that $D$ achieves the minimum.

This is a problem at the intersection of spectral geometry, algorithmic
information theory, and noncommutative geometry. Expertise in Connes'
noncommutative geometry programme \cite{connes1996} is directly relevant, as
the spectral triple formalism provides much of the required technical machinery.

\subsection{Robust and representation-independent proof-of-concept simulations}

The current proof-of-concept simulations use Gaussian wave packets as observer
proxies and a Metropolis-Hastings sampler to explore configuration space. While
the results are encouraging, three limitations must be addressed.

First, the interpretation space of even small bitstrings is super-exponentially
large. The current implementation explores only a tiny fraction of the
available configurations. More efficient sampling strategies --- possibly
inspired by tensor network methods or variational inference --- are needed to
obtain results that are robust against sampling bias.

Second, the current simulations demonstrate qualitative emergence of inertia,
interference, and attraction. The next target is quantitative agreement: the
emergent dynamics should reproduce specific numerical predictions of quantum
mechanics and general relativity, not merely their qualitative character.

Third, the lognormal microstructure result (Section~\ref{sec:emergence},
Prediction~7) should be tested across a wider range of geometries and filter
types, and the fixed-point filter should be identified computationally. The
fixed-point filter is the one whose shape parameters under the hierarchical
recursion satisfy a self-consistency condition, and its identification would
constitute a derivation of the particle spectrum from the compression principle.

\subsection{Fermionic exclusion from collision-and-rebound simulations}

As described in Section~\ref{sec:open}, the emergence of fermionic exclusion
from the description-length explosion upon observer merger is sketched but not
demonstrated. The immediate experimental target is a simulation of two
observer-proxy blobs on a collision course, in which the expected outcome is
not merger (bosonic) but rebound with preserved identity (fermionic). If the
sharp short-range repulsion predicted by the description-length argument
emerges naturally from the compression measure, without any antisymmetry being
hard-coded, Prediction~5 is confirmed and the framework's particle physics
programme becomes tractable.

\section{Discussion and Relation to Existing Frameworks}
\label{sec:discussion}

\textbf{Relation to relational quantum mechanics.}
Rovelli's relational quantum mechanics \cite{rovelli1996} similarly holds that
physical quantities are observer-relative. The \iame{} goes further: the
observer and its universe are identical ($O = U_O$), and the relational
structure is derived from a compression measure rather than postulated.

\textbf{Relation to the holographic principle.}
The holographic principle \cite{thooft1993, susskind1995} holds that the
information content of a region is encoded on its boundary. This is consistent
with the \iame{}'s geometric compression scheme $\Cg$, which minimises boundary
description length. The entropy decrease of collapsing spacetime --- reaching
zero at the singularity --- is consistent with the holographic trend and
provides a possible information-theoretic derivation of why holography holds.

\textbf{Relation to Barbour's timeless physics.}
Barbour \cite{barbour1999} argues from general relativity that time is not
fundamental and the universe is a static ``Platonia'' of configurations. The
\iame{} reaches the same conclusion from the opposite direction --- from the
observer inward --- and provides a measure over configurations that Barbour's
framework lacks. Penrose's Weyl curvature hypothesis similarly assumes an
initially smooth state, but requires quantum mechanics and spacetime as
primitives. The \iame{} generates smooth initial states from a perfectly
zero-entropy bitstring without these assumptions.

\textbf{Relation to Connes' noncommutative geometry.}
Connes' spectral action programme \cite{connes1996} derives the Einstein-Hilbert
action and the Standard Model action from a single spectral triple. The \iame{}
provides a motivation that Connes' approach lacks: the spectral action is
selected not by mathematical elegance but by the compression measure. The
Dirac operator wins because it is the shortest description of spin geometry.
Connes derives the same functional from different starting axioms; the \iame{}
explains why those axioms hold.

\textbf{Relation to Solomonoff induction and algorithmic information theory.}
The measure $P(c) \propto 2^{-\Ctotal}$ is a variant of the universal prior
of Solomonoff \cite{solomonoff1964}. The \iame{} applies this measure not to
prediction sequences but to observer-conditioned configuration spaces, and
extends it with a dual spectral-geometric decomposition motivated by the
structure of observed physics.

\textbf{What is genuinely new.}
The \iame{}'s distinctive contributions are: (1) the derivation of $O = U_O$
from the IEP without additional assumptions; (2) the joint spectral-geometric
compressibility equation as a single informational Lagrangian from which both
QM and GR emerge as complementary schemes; (3) the conjecture that this
two-term functional is the scale decomposition of a single Dirac spectral
action, providing a route to full spectral unification; (4) the natural
suppression of Boltzmann brains by the same measure that selects physical
structure; (5) the assignment of qualia a functional, non-epiphenomenal role;
and (6) the derivation of an arrow-of-time and singularity-regularisation from
pure informational principles.
