\begin{abstract}
  We propose an observer centric static fully self-contained informational framework in which physical law emerges as
  the most probable consequences. Observers are coherent substructures whose histories are weighted by 
  different measures: discrete, geometric and spectral complexity.
  Quantum mechanics and general relativity arise as complementary compression mechanisms.
  Hilbert-space structure provides an optimal spectral encoding of microscopic correlations,
  while geometry provides an optimal encoding of macroscopic structures with well defined boundaries.
  Observers therefore most probably find themselves in histories where both spectral and geometric compression are simultaneously near-optimal.
\end{abstract}

\noindent \textbf{Keywords:} Spectral Complexity, Minimal Description Length, Emergent Gravity, Observer-Conditioned Typicality
