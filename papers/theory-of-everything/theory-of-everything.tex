\begin{abstract}
We present \iame{}, a
candidate unified framework in which physical laws, spacetime geometry, quantum
behaviour, and mathematics are not fundamental postulates but emergent
consequences of a single informational variational principle. The sole primitive
is the existence of a finite, structured observer. From this, and without
assuming discreteness, finiteness, spacetime, or any informational ontology at
the base level, we derive: (i) that the universe is fundamentally static and
time is an internal ordinal relation; (ii) that the observer and the universe it
experiences are identical structures ($O = U_O$); (iii) that observed physical
laws are the large-deviation minimisers of a joint spectral-geometric
description-length cost functional $\Linfo = \Cs + \Cg$; and (iv) that quantum
mechanics and general relativity arise as complementary compression schemes
selected by this functional. The framework provides a natural suppression of
Boltzmann-brain fluctuations, resolves the Wigner effectiveness of mathematics,
and assigns qualia a functional non-epiphenomenal role as heuristic navigators
in configuration space. Proof-of-concept simulations demonstrate that inertia,
wave interference, and gravitational attraction emerge from pure compression
optimisation without hard-coded physical laws. The outstanding open problem ---
the natural emergence of fermionic exclusion from the compression measure ---
is identified and its resolution sketched. Full simulation details are provided
in the Supplementary Material.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Every candidate unified framework faces a foundational question: what is
assumed, and what is derived? String theory assumes extended objects propagating
in a background spacetime. Loop quantum gravity assumes a discrete spin-network
structure. The Standard Model assumes a specific gauge group and particle
content. Each framework imports considerable structure at the outset and then
derives consequences.

The framework takes a different route. We begin with the
minimum possible primitive: the bare existence of a conscious, finite observer.
We then ask what physics must look like, given only that starting point and
the requirement that observer-compatible configurations be statistically
typical.

The answer, we argue, is our observed universe.

Physical laws, quantum mechanics, spacetime geometry, the arrow of time, the
effectiveness of mathematics, and the suppression of Boltzmann-brain
fluctuations all follow as derived consequences --- not fine-tuned accidents ---
of a single informational variational principle. The framework is not an
interpretation of existing physics; it is a derivation of physics from
something more fundamental than physics.

The paper is structured as follows. Section~\ref{sec:axioms} presents the
axiomatic chain from which the framework is derived. Section~\ref{sec:iep}
states the Internal Emergence Principle and its immediate consequences.
Section~\ref{sec:ontology} develops the ontological position.
Section~\ref{sec:measure} presents the measure theory and the joint
compressibility equation. Section~\ref{sec:emergence} derives the emergent
structure of physics. Section~\ref{sec:predictions} states the framework's
falsifiable predictions. Section~\ref{sec:open} discusses the open problem of
fermionic exclusion. Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes.

\section{The Axiomatic Chain}
\label{sec:axioms}

We derive the framework through a sequence of propositions, each of which can
be independently accepted or rejected. Rejecting any node commits the rejector
to a specific alternative, which we state explicitly.

\begin{axiom}[Existence of the observer]
\label{ax:exist}
Something is experiencing. This cannot be coherently denied, because the denial
is itself performed by the entity whose existence is being denied.
\end{axiom}

This is the only strictly self-evident claim available. It requires no
inference. It is a direct datum.

\begin{axiom}[Finitude of the observer]
\label{ax:finite}
The observer's memory, attention, and lifespan are bounded. This follows from
direct observation of the observer by the observer, without inference about
the external universe.
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[DNA as the mechanism of conscious observers]
\label{ax:dna}
DNA, operating within the observed laws of physics, is the only mechanism we
have ever observed that reliably produces conscious, pain-sensitive observers.
This is an empirical claim supported by all available biological evidence.
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[Physical closure]
\label{ax:closure}
DNA is composed entirely of ordinary physical matter obeying ordinary physical
laws. No non-physical process plays any role in its operation. No experiment
has ever required invoking anything beyond standard physics and chemistry to
explain DNA's operation.
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[Computability of DNA processes]
\label{ax:compute}
The physical processes underlying DNA fall within the computable. A
sufficiently powerful computer could, in principle, simulate them. We require
only that the specific class of processes DNA relies on is computable --- a
weaker claim than the full Church-Turing thesis.
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[Causal efficacy of qualia]
\label{ax:qualia}
Pain is not epiphenomenal. A system experiencing pain and the same system
without pain are physically distinguishable in principle:
$\mathrm{Human} \neq \mathrm{Human} + \mathrm{Pain}$.
Rejecting this commits one to accepting philosophical zombies.
\end{axiom}

\begin{proposition}[Simulation consciousness]
\label{prop:sim}
A simulation physically indistinguishable from a conscious being must itself
be conscious.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Suppose a simulation $S$ is physically indistinguishable from a conscious
system $C$. If $S$ were not conscious, the experiential difference would
produce a measurable behavioural difference, contradicting the assumption of
indistinguishability. By Axiom~\ref{ax:qualia}, pain has measurable effects.
Therefore $S$ must be conscious. \qed
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Static bitstring equivalence]
\label{prop:static}
A fully static bitstring --- the precomputed execution trace of a simulation
--- contains the same conscious experience as the running simulation. Active
execution is not required.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Suppose experience vanishes when the last line of running code is replaced by
precomputed data. Then there exists a code-to-data threshold below which
consciousness ceases. Such a threshold would constitute a new physical constant
with no basis in Axioms~\ref{ax:closure}--\ref{ax:qualia}. Its introduction
requires a non-physical explanatory principle, contradicting
Axiom~\ref{ax:closure}. Therefore no such threshold exists, and the static
bitstring is experientially equivalent to the running simulation. \qed
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Combinatorial containment of consciousness]
\label{prop:combinatorial}
Any sufficiently large configuration space contains conscious observers,
with no simulation, process, or external reader required.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
A conscious observer is, by Propositions~\ref{prop:sim} and~\ref{prop:static},
a finite bitstring of length $k$. By combinatorics, every bitstring of length
$k$ appears within any sufficiently large set of bitstrings. The question
``who is running it?'' has no more purchase than asking ``who is running the
number seven?'' \qed
\end{proof}

\section{The Internal Emergence Principle}
\label{sec:iep}

The three propositions above jointly constitute the \textbf{Internal Emergence
Principle} (IEP):

\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Internal Emergence Principle]
Consciousness is a property of certain finite information structures, not of
processes acting upon them. Any configuration space large enough to contain
such structures contains consciousness.
\end{tcolorbox}

The IEP has six immediate structural consequences.

\subsection{The universe is fundamentally static}

The IEP establishes that a conscious observer exists within a static bitstring
with no external process required. Therefore no external clock is required for
experience to occur. Time within experience must be defined by internal
structure alone. The only available internal ordering is:

\begin{equation}
c_2 \succ_O c_1 \iff c_2 \text{ contains a memory of } c_1
\end{equation}

Time is an ordinal index over observer configurations. The universe, at the
level of the totality, is static.

This conclusion is independently supported by the block-universe interpretation
of general relativity, in which the four-dimensional spacetime manifold is a
static geometric object and the ``flow'' of time has no representation in the
Einstein field equations \cite{barbour1999}.

\subsection{The observer-universe relationship is representational}

A computer running a DNA simulation of Alice and Alice experiencing pain are
two descriptions of the same static information. Causality requires time
ordering. A static structure has no intrinsic time ordering. Therefore the
relationship between physical substrate and observer experience is
\emph{representational}, not causal. Neither perspective is more fundamental.

\subsection{Configuration multiplicity}

A bitstring of length $n$ admits exactly $2^n$ possible configurations. One
describes Alice's universe as she experiences it. One describes the physical
computer. The remaining $2^n - 2$ are other valid configurations of the same
information --- most producing no coherent observer, some producing other
observers entirely. No configuration is privileged over another by the axioms.

\subsection{Internal containment of observations}

In a static universe with no running external process, no mechanism exists by
which external bits could alter internal bits. Therefore every structure Alice
experiences --- every star, every sensation --- must already be encoded within
the bitstring that constitutes her. Alice does not receive information. She
discovers structure already present within her.

\subsection{The universe as interior}

From the containment result: every structure Alice experiences is pre-encoded
in her structure. The universe Alice experiences is not her container. It is
her interior. Alice does not live inside the universe. The universe lives
inside Alice.

\subsection{Observer-universe identity: $O = U_O$}

Combining the standard self-model (Alice $\subset U_{\mathrm{Alice}}$) with
the containment result ($U_{\mathrm{Alice}} \subset$ Alice) and applying basic
set theory ($A \subset B$ and $B \subset A$ implies $A = B$):

\begin{equation}
\boxed{O = U_O}
\end{equation}

For every observer $O$, the observer and the universe it experiences are
identical structures. There is no view from nowhere. What two observers share
is the intersection of their universes:
$U_{O_1} \cap U_{O_2}$ --- the shared compressible structure that constitutes
intersubjective, verifiable physics.

\section{Ontology: What Fundamentally Exists}
\label{sec:ontology}

\subsection{The unrestricted totality}

We do not assume finiteness. If reality were fundamentally finite, one could
immediately ask: finite by how much? Why $n$ bits rather than $n+1$? Any fixed
finite limit appears contingent and calls for a deeper explanatory layer.

We also do not assume information as primitive. Information presupposes
distinguishability --- at its simplest, binary distinction. But declaring
reality to be fundamentally informational or digital is itself a substantive
ontological commitment that nothing prior licenses.

We therefore do not presuppose that reality is fundamentally finite, discrete,
informational, mathematical, or governed by prior external laws.

We denote this unrestricted, pre-interpretive totality $\mathbf{U}$. We do
not claim to know what it is --- only that no specific formatting principle is
granted ontological privilege a priori.

\subsection{The observer constraint}

Despite radical agnosticism about the substrate, one empirical fact remains:
a conscious, structured, finite observer exists (Axioms~\ref{ax:exist}
and~\ref{ax:finite}). This is the boundary condition imposed on $\mathbf{U}$.

The minimal ontological assumption is therefore:

\begin{equation}
\text{Undefined } \mathbf{U} \;\longrightarrow\; \text{Finite Observer}
\end{equation}

The step remains unexplained. That is an explicit boundary of the theory.

\subsection{The guitar string analogy}

A guitar string is perfectly continuous. Discreteness --- the resonant modes,
the quantised frequencies --- emerges from the boundary conditions imposed by
clamping the string at both ends. The continuous medium manifests discrete
states not because discreteness is ontologically primitive but because boundary
conditions force it.

Likewise, a finite observer interacting with an unrestricted reality induces
discrete informational structure without discreteness being ontologically
primitive. Under Solomonoff-style measures, low-complexity, highly compressible
structures dominate typicality. Finite observers therefore find themselves
embedded within stable, structured, compressible equivalence classes.

Information is not what reality is made of. Information is what finite
observers perceive when they interact with an otherwise unrestricted reality.

\section{Measure: What is Typical}
\label{sec:measure}

\subsection{Configuration space}

For formal descriptive purposes, we introduce a finite, static configuration
space:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{C} = \{0,1\}^n
\end{equation}
where each $c \in \mathcal{C}$ is a complete, static snapshot of bits. The
binary system is chosen for convenience; it carries no ontological commitment
to digitalism.

Each configuration $c$ admits multiple potential descriptions
$d \in \mathcal{D}(c)$. We quantify the total complexity $\Ctotal(d)$ by
splitting it into dual spectral and geometric components:
\begin{equation}
\Ctotal(d) = \Cs(d) + \Cg(d)
\end{equation}

\subsection{Spectral and geometric complexity}

The spectral complexity $\Cs(d)$ measures the algorithmic bit-cost required to
track wave-like modes:
\begin{equation}
\Cs(d) = \sum_i \left[
  \mathrm{Cost}(A_i) + \mathrm{Cost}(\phi_i) +
  \frac{\omega_i}{\Delta\omega}
\right]
\end{equation}
where the sum runs over active spectral modes, $\mathrm{Cost}(A_i)$ is the
bit-depth of the amplitude envelope, $\mathrm{Cost}(\phi_i)$ is the
fixed-width register encoding the phase interval $[0, 2\pi]$, and
$\omega_i/\Delta\omega$ is the linear resource allocation required to track the
mode frequency relative to minimum resolution $\Delta\omega$.

The geometric complexity $\Cg(d)$ accounts for localised spatial structures,
boundaries, and metric fields.

\subsection{The joint compressibility equation}

Because the spectral and geometric representations form a many-to-many mapping
--- any observer wavefunction can be interpreted through nearly infinitely many
spacetime metrics, and conversely --- the joint probability of a physical state
$s$ is the product of its individual probabilities:

\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\Pjoint(s)
= P_{\mathrm{spectral}}(s) \times P_{\mathrm{geometric}}(s)
= 2^{-\Cs(s)} \times 2^{-\Cg(s)}
= 2^{-(\Cs(s) + \Cg(s))}
}
\end{equation}

This yields the informational Lagrangian:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:lagrangian}
\Linfo = \Cs(s) + \Cg(s) \;\longrightarrow\; \text{minimum}
\end{equation}

Neither geometry nor waves are primary. They are equal, co-dependent
projections of a single underlying informational structure. Observed reality
is the sweet spot of this joint optimisation.

\subsection{Induced measure and observer functionals}

The raw algorithmic weight of a static configuration $c$ is accumulated across
all its valid descriptions:
\begin{equation}
W(c) = \sum_{d \in \mathcal{D}(c)} 2^{-\Ctotal(d)}
\end{equation}

This defines the raw baseline probability distribution:
\begin{equation}
P(c) = \frac{W(c)}{\sum_{c' \in \mathcal{C}} W(c')}
\end{equation}

An observer is modelled as a grading semantic functional:
\begin{equation}
\muO : \mathcal{C} \to [0,1]
\end{equation}
which measures how strongly a given configuration describes observer-relative
structure, inducing an equivalence relation $c_1 \sim_O c_2$ whenever $c_1$
and $c_2$ describe the same relational observer structure to within the
tolerances encoded by $\muO$.

The observer-conditioned probability is:
\begin{equation}
P(c \mid O) = \frac{\muO(c)\,W(c)}{\sum_{c'} \muO(c')\,W(c')}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Probability of an observed history}

To transition from the probability of a single configuration to the probability
of an entire experienced history, we track how an observer traverses the
configuration space. Let $\gamma \in \Gamma_O$ be a path --- a sequence of
configurations ordered by the internal temporal relation $\prec_O$.

Because configurations along the path are independent slices of the static
totality, their algorithmic costs sum additively. Converting from bits to
natural units ($2^{-C} = e^{-C\ln 2}$):

\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\mathbb{P}(\gamma \mid O)
= \frac{1}{Z_O} \exp\!\left(-\lambda\,\mathcal{C}_O[\gamma]\right),
\qquad \gamma \in \Gamma_O
}
\end{equation}

where $\mathcal{C}_O[\gamma] = \int_\gamma \Ctotal(c)\,dt$ is the accumulated
informational cost along the path and $\lambda$ acts as an algorithmic inverse
temperature scaling the observer's filtering acuity. Observed physical laws are
the large-deviation minimisers of $\mathcal{C}_O[\gamma]$ over the set of all
observer-compatible paths.

\section{Emergence: The Structure of Physics}
\label{sec:emergence}

\subsection{The D-$\psi$-G trinity}

Three emergent layers of reality arise from the joint compressibility
minimisation:

\begin{equation}
D - \psi - G
\end{equation}

\begin{itemize}
  \item $\mathbf{D}$ \textbf{(Data/Description):} Raw, discrete digital
        information --- manifesting physically as localised particles.
  \item $\boldsymbol{\psi}$ \textbf{(Spectral/Quantum):} The smooth wave-codec
        that compresses the relational possibility space, minimising $\Cs$.
  \item $\mathbf{G}$ \textbf{(Geometry/Gravity):} The continuous metric
        manifold that compresses the realisation space at scale, minimising
        $\Cg$.
\end{itemize}

Discrete particles are localised, high-density ``knots'' where the smooth
continuous wave-codec meets the finite digital register of the observer.
Spacetime is smooth ($G$), fields are continuous ($\psi$), yet observations
are invariably quantised into discrete particles ($D$) --- because $D$ is the
emergent \emph{output} of joint minimisation when parsed by a bounded
interpreter.

\subsection{Emergence of spacetime}

Observer-compatible configurations are dominated by those exhibiting inertia,
smooth curved paths, and law-like behaviour, because sharp, chaotic particle
trajectories require massive high-frequency modes that exponentially spike
$\Cs$ and are suppressed by the joint measure.

Spacetime geometry emerges as an internal ordering relation $\prec_O$ on
configurations:
\begin{equation}
c_1 \prec_O c_2 \iff c_2 \text{ supports the observer functional } \muO
\end{equation}

The experienced flow of time corresponds to traversal along the most
compressible, optimised paths through the timeless configuration space.

\subsection{Quantum mechanics and general relativity as dual compression schemes}

The central structural claim of the framework is that quantum mechanics
(spectral/wave description) and general relativity (geometric description) are
not fundamental, independent theories. They are the two complementary
compression schemes that minimise total description length for persistent,
finite observers:

\begin{itemize}
  \item $\psi$ minimises $\Cs$ --- the bit-cost of encoding relational
        possibility and smooth, extended interactions.
  \item $G$ minimises $\Cg$ --- the bit-cost of encoding locality, boundaries,
        and macroscopic identity.
\end{itemize}

Their unification is therefore not a technical problem of quantising spacetime.
It is an already-solved structural consequence of the joint minimisation
(\ref{eq:lagrangian}). The framework predicts that any correct theory of
quantum gravity will take the form of a joint spectral-geometric minimisation
principle rather than a quantisation of a background metric.

\subsection{Emergence of laws and mathematics}

Physical laws and mathematics emerge as stable relational invariants within the
high-measure compressible slices that observers self-locate into. Wigner's
``unreasonable effectiveness of mathematics'' \cite{wigner1960} follows because
observers and the mathematics they discover are carved from the same compressible
structure: both are low-complexity patterns within $\mathbf{U}$.

\subsection{Qualia as heuristic navigators}

The full spectrum of qualia --- pain, pleasure, intuition --- is the
first-person, phenomenological signature of the observer functional $\muO$
navigating the interpretive landscape toward highly compressible, stable
structures.

This is not epiphenomenalism. Qualia have the causal efficacy required by
Axiom~\ref{ax:qualia}: they are the felt steering mechanism that guides finite
observers toward high-measure paths without exhaustive search. They are what it
feels like to be a highly probable, compressible observer. The hard problem of
consciousness is dissolved rather than solved: qualia require no separate
ontological category because they are identical to the heuristic navigation
process itself.

\subsection{Suppression of Boltzmann brains}

Raw high-entropy noise and transient fluctuations have high total description
length and are exponentially disfavoured by $\Pjoint$. Typical observers
inhabit low-initial-entropy trajectories with stable D-$\psi$-G structure.
Boltzmann-brain fluctuations are suppressed not by fine-tuning but by the
same compression measure that selects all other physical structure.

\section{Falsifiable Predictions}
\label{sec:predictions}

The framework makes a number of specific predictions distinguishable from
those of existing theories.

\textbf{Prediction 1: Dual structure is necessary.}
Any stable observer-hosting physics must exhibit both wave-like ($\psi$) and
geometric ($G$) structure. A universe with only one of the two --- pure waves
without geometry, or pure geometry without quantum fields --- is forbidden by
the joint compressibility requirement. This constrains the solution space of
any candidate unified theory.

\textbf{Prediction 2: Discreteness is never primitive.}
The framework predicts that any deeper physical theory will not be
fundamentally discrete. Discreteness emerges at the observer boundary. This
distinguishes the framework from strict digital physics (e.g.\ cellular
automaton or hypergraph approaches) and constitutes a falsifiable fork: if a
future theory demonstrates that fundamental discreteness is unavoidable, the
framework is refuted.

\textbf{Prediction 3: Quantum gravity as informational minimisation.}
The correct theory of quantum gravity will take the form of a joint
spectral-geometric minimisation principle rather than a quantisation of
spacetime. Loop quantum gravity and string theory both treat spacetime as in
some sense fundamental. The \iame{} predicts both are approximations to a deeper
informational structure.

\textbf{Prediction 4: Low-entropy past without fine-tuning.}
The framework predicts a low-entropy past as the \emph{expected} outcome of
the compression measure, not a lucky accident requiring anthropic selection.
This is a stronger claim than standard cosmology makes and is in principle
distinguishable by its derivation of the arrow of time without additional
assumptions.

\textbf{Prediction 5: Fermionic exclusion from compression.}
The Pauli exclusion principle must emerge naturally from the observer
functional $\muO$ as a consequence of description-length explosion upon
observer merger (see Section~\ref{sec:open}). If no such mechanism can be
identified within the compression measure, the framework is incomplete. This
is a precise, binary, testable claim.

\textbf{Prediction 6: Macro-micro scaling of interference.}
At macroscopic scales, fine interference structure averages out and observers
tend toward smooth classical trajectories. At microscopic scales, wave-like
behaviour dominates. This scaling behaviour --- already observed in
proof-of-concept simulations --- should reproduce the quantum-to-classical
transition quantitatively, not merely qualitatively.

A minimal 2+1D proof-of-concept simulation was constructed to test whether

configuration space guided exclusively by minimisation of $\Ctotal = \Cs + \Cg$.

\textbf{Results:} Emergent inertia, wave interference patterns, and weak
mutual attraction (a primitive form of gravity) all appear consistently without
being programmed. The macroscopic and microscopic scaling behaviours are
qualitatively consistent with Prediction~6. Full simulation details, figures,
and video supplementary materials are provided in the Supplementary Material.

These results support the thesis that law-like behaviour is not imposed from
outside but arises internally as the consequence of how self-interpretive
information is most efficiently organised.

\section{The Open Problem: Fermionic Exclusion}
\label{sec:open}

The most important open challenge is the natural emergence of fermionic
exclusion statistics from the compression measure.

In the current simulations, Gaussian observer blobs eventually lose their
individual identities and merge. Symmetric (bosonic) configurations are
strongly preferred because they allow shared spectral modes and lower total
description length. Antisymmetric configurations are expensive and suppressed.
Hard-coding antisymmetry would violate the spirit of the project.

We sketch the resolution as follows. Two independent observers $O_1$ and $O_2$
are each separately compressible: their internal structures are smooth,
coherent, and self-consistent. When they attempt to occupy the same state, the
merged description must encode both internal structures simultaneously in the
same region. These structures are not correlated --- they were independently
optimised. The result is analogous to injecting frames from one video stream
into another: the combined description length does not sum as
$C(O_1) + C(O_2)$ but explodes, because the two compression schemes interfere
destructively.

Formally, the observer functional $\muO$ must assign near-zero measure to
configurations in which a previously distinct observer ceases to be
distinguishable. An observer that loses its distinctness ceases to satisfy the
observer boundary condition --- it is no longer a finite, structured,
persistent observer in the sense of Axioms~\ref{ax:exist}--\ref{ax:finite}.

The exclusion principle then follows not from an external antisymmetry rule but
from the stability-of-identity condition on $\muO$: merger is the least
probable configuration because it maximally violates the observer's
self-consistency requirement.

The predicted signature is an effective short-range repulsion that is sharper
and more localised than the emergent long-range attraction, because attraction
arises from gradual wavefunction overlap (smooth, cheap) while repulsion arises
from structural incompatibility (sharp, expensive). This asymmetry ---
long-range attraction, short-range repulsion --- is precisely what stable
matter requires. Demonstrating this computationally in a collision-and-rebound
