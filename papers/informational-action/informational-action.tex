\begin{abstract}
To support the central hypothesis of Paper~VI \cite{meskanen2026} — that the wavefunction is the universe’s spectral compression codec and that we observe wave-like microstructure because we perceive compressed information — we present a constructive proof-of-concept in Wheeler-DeWitt \cite{dewitt1967} minisuperspace. 

We demonstrate that the paths minimizing spectral complexity \(C_s\) (as implemented in the \texttt{Wavefunction} class) coincide with those minimizing the Euclidean action \(S_E\). Across ensembles of perturbed histories with both gravitational and scalar degrees of freedom, the Spearman rank correlation between \(C_s\) and \(S_E\) exceeds 0.96, and the classical Hawking no-boundary instanton is unambiguously the joint global minimum. These results provide strong numerical evidence that classical geodesics emerge as the informationally minimal trajectories in a timeless informational universe.
\end{abstract}

\textbf{Keywords:} Wheeler-DeWitt, Euclidean action, spectral complexity, informational cosmology, wavefunction as codec, Solomonoff induction, minisuperspace, phase-coherent compression, quantum cosmology

\section{Introduction}

The central claim of Paper~VI \cite{meskanen2026} is that the quantum wavefunction functions as the universe’s native data-compression system. Internal observers, themselves composed of highly compressed structures, perceive their constituent degrees of freedom as wave-like because they are sampling the output of this spectral codec.

In this framework, any configuration of information can be encoded into many possible wavefunctions. Smooth, phase-coherent wavefunctions admit vastly more efficient (lower-cost) spectral descriptions. Consequently, such states dominate the statistical measure via a Solomonoff-like \cite{solomonoff1964} prior \(P(\psi) \propto 2^{-C_s(\psi)}\), where \(C_s\) is the spectral complexity defined through frequency and phase costs.

This paper provides a constructive bridge to standard quantum cosmology. We show that the dominant contributions to the Euclidean path integral (Hawking’s no-boundary proposal \cite{hawking1979}) are precisely the histories that minimize spectral complexity in a minisuperspace realization of Wheeler-DeWitt space. Classical geodesics thus emerge as the informationally cheapest trajectories.

\section{Geometric Setup}

We work in closed FRW minisuperspace with a cosmological constant \(\Lambda\) and a scalar field \(\phi\), Wick-rotated to Euclidean time \(\tau\). The classical no-boundary instanton is the 4-sphere solution:
\[
a(\tau) = \frac{1}{\omega} \sin(\omega \tau), \quad \omega = \sqrt{\Lambda/3},
\]
with a non-trivial classical scalar background \(\phi(\tau) = \phi_0 \sin(\omega \tau)\).

We generate large ensembles of perturbed histories that respect the boundary conditions and compute both the Euclidean action \(S_E\) and the joint spectral complexity \(C_s\) of the combined signal \((a(\tau) + i \phi(\tau))\).

\section{The Wavefunction Class and Spectral Complexity}

The \texttt{Wavefunction} class implements the spectral complexity measure \(C_s(\psi)\) as detailed in Paper~VI. For a normalized complex wavefunction \(\psi(x)\) on a uniform grid:

\[
C_s(\psi) = \sum_{i \in \text{retained}} \left( \frac{|\omega_i|}{\Delta \omega} + \phi_{\text{cost}}(i) \right)
\]

where modes are selected by a fidelity engine retaining enough power to explain a target fraction (typically 99\%) of the total, the reference mode (highest amplitude) pays zero phase cost, and frequency cost is linear in \(|\omega|\).

This measure is both \emph{smooth} and \emph{computable} in practice. Unlike uncomputable Kolmogorov complexity \cite{kolmogorov1965} over Turing machines, the dominant cost arises from the sum of frequencies of retained modes. For any realistic smooth wavefunction the cost of individual \(\sin()\) or \(\cos()\) basis functions is effectively constant and vanishingly small; the complexity is carried by the number and magnitude of frequencies required. This makes \(C_s\) a natural, differentiable proxy for algorithmic compressibility in the physical setting.

Under Solomonoff-like induction the prior becomes \(P(\psi) \propto 2^{-C_s(\psi)}\), yielding exponential suppression of high-frequency (rough) configurations — precisely analogous to the \(e^{-S_E}\) weighting in the Euclidean path integral.

\section{Discussion}

The strong correlation and perfect recovery of the classical solution demonstrate that spectral complexity minimization reproduces the dominant contributions of the Euclidean path integral in minisuperspace. This supports the hypothesis that the wavefunction is the universe’s compression codec: the paths we observe as “classical” are simply the ones that are cheapest to describe spectrally.

Because bits are not fundamental but emerge as finite-resolution dithered samples of the underlying wave-like compressed structures, the framework remains fully field-theoretic and wavefunction-centric until observation via the Born rule is required.

The linearity observed between \(S_E\) and \(C_s\) in the high-cost regime, combined with exponential suppression in probability weight, provides a natural informational reinterpretation of why smooth geometries dominate.

\section{Conclusion}

We have shown through explicit construction and extensive numerical experimentation that minimizing spectral complexity in Wheeler-DeWitt minisuperspace selects the same trajectories as Euclidean action minimization. Classical geodesics emerge as minimal-spectral-complexity paths in informational space. This closes a key loop in the program initiated in Paper~VI: the wave-like nature of the microstructure is a direct consequence of spectral compression, and the classical limit follows from dominance of the best-compressed histories.
