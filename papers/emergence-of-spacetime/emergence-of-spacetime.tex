\begin{abstract}
  We present a purely statistical, information-theoretic model that duplicates the
  inflationary and accelerating evolution of the universe without any hard-coded physical laws,
  equations of motion, metric tensors, or imposed initial irregularities.
  Beginning under the \iame{} framework from a perfectly smooth, zero-entropy bitstring—the
  unique all-zero state—we evolve the system through random bit-flip mutations
  that incrementally increase Shannon entropy, interpreting each state relationally as a spatial configuration.
  As entropy increases, three striking phenomena emerge natively from the underlying combinatorics:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item An exponential spatial expansion originating from a single structureless point.
  \item Hierarchical particle-like matter spectrum whose emergent abundance follows a non-monotonic lognormal distribution.
  \item A late-stage transition into an accelerating cosmic expansion characterized by a positive Hubble constant,
    driven entirely by the relational metric transformations of an embedded observer as higher-order macro-structures decay.
  \end{itemize}
  The universality of these results across distinct geometric decoding schemes and non-embedded
  pattern-matching representations demonstrates that this evolution is an intrinsic property of entropy-increasing configurations,
  rather than an artifact of a specific filter design. We conclude that cosmic expansion is the geometric
  manifestation of increasing informational entropy.
  This result provides a foundational resolution to the initial conditions problem:
  no residual quantum scale irregularities are required to generate a rich, persistent, and accelerating universe.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Paper~II showed that gravitational collapse, analysed through the execution
trace of a computational simulation, converges to a zero-entropy state
at the classical singularity. The geometric description diverges; the
informational description converges to the simplest possible state. The
singularity is not a pathological endpoint but a geometric point.

The present paper asks what happens when we begin at that geometric point
and move in the opposite direction. Starting from the unique zero-entropy state
--- the all-zero bitstring, the same informational state that
corresponds to the singularity --- and increasing entropy through random
mutations, what geometric and structural features emerge? Do they resemble
those of the observed universe?

The central hypothesis is that cosmic expansion and the formation of
structure are, from the informational perspective of the \iame{} framework,
the geometric interpretation of a monotonically increasing entropy process.
Entropy increase is not a consequence of expansion; expansion is the
geometric reading of entropy increase. The two are the same phenomenon seen
through different lenses.

This hypothesis has a direct implication for the initial conditions problem.
A longstanding puzzle in cosmology is why the universe began in such an
exceptionally ordered state. Standard approaches, including Penrose's Weyl
curvature hypothesis \cite{penrose1979}, assume that the initial state was
smooth only within the limits imposed by quantum uncertainty: some residual
irregularity at the quantum scale is taken as necessary. In the present
framework, this assumption is unnecessary. The initial state is perfectly
smooth by construction --- zero entropy, uniquely determined, admitting no
fluctuations of any kind. The richness of the universe that emerges from it
is not a consequence of imposed irregularities but of entropy increase alone.

The paper is structured as follows. Section~\ref{sec:setup} describes the
computational setup, the geometric and combinatorial filter definitions, and
the role of bit-flip mutations. Section~\ref{sec:results} presents the
numerical results. Section~\ref{sec:robustness} establishes the
filter-independence of the lognormal result. Section~\ref{sec:discussion}
interprets the findings in the context of the \iame{} framework and compares
them with existing cosmological approaches. Section~\ref{sec:conclusion}
concludes.

\section{Setup}
\label{sec:setup}

\subsection{Configuration Space and Entropy Evolution}

The system is defined by a binary configuration string $S_t$ of fixed total length $L$. The initial state $S_0$ corresponds to a unique, completely uniform zero-entropy state (the all-zero bitstring), establishing an initial boundary condition where Shannon entropy is minimized:
\[
H(S_0) = 0
\]
We evolve the configuration via discrete, random bit-flip mutations. Within the timeless ontology of the \iame{} framework, these mutations do not represent a fundamental dynamical law or a temporal arrow; rather, they serve as a stochastic sampling probe traversing a static ensemble of $2^L$ available configurations along approximately entropy-increasing trajectories, conceptually allied with the Page-Wootters formalism.

The instantaneous Shannon entropy of the system at any given evolutionary checkpoint is expressed as:
\[
H(S_t) = -p_0(t)\log_2 p_0(t) - p_1(t)\log_2 p_1(t)
\]
where $p_0(t)$ and $p_1(t)$ represent the normalized empirical frequencies of $0$- and $1$-bits across the global string $S_t$.

\subsection{Recursive Spatial Fabric and Structural Filtering}

To construct a relational space from a non-embedded bitstring, an internal observer applies a multi-scale cascading filter. Space is not postulated a priori; it is derived from the density of structural matches.

\subsubsection{Level-0: The Spacetime Fabric (Graviton Scale)}
We define a local target pattern string $P$ of length $w_0$. The global string $S_t$ is parsed into non-overlapping blocks of size $w_0$, yielding a raw background capacity of $N_{max} = L / w_0$ elements. A Level-0 ``graviton'' token exists at index $m$ if the block matches the binary target integer value $V_P$:
\[
\sigma_G[m] = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } \sum_{j=0}^{w_0-1} S_t[m \cdot w_0 + j] \cdot 2^{w_0 - 1 - j} = V_P \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
\]
The raw count of detected space fabric elements is $N_G = \sum \sigma_G[m]$.

\subsubsection{Level-$k$: Hierarchical Matter Sub-Scales}
Higher-order structural tiers (representing Hadrons, Atoms, and Compounds sequentially) are extracted by recursively filtering the underlying layer's output array. For a given structural level $k \in \{1, 2, 3\}$, the input array is scanned using a non-overlapping window of width $w_k$ subject to a density activation threshold $\tau_k$:
\[
\sigma_k[m] = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } \sum_{j=0}^{w_k-1} \sigma_{k-1}[m \cdot w_k + j] \geq \tau_k \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
\]
The raw abundance of entities at level $k$ is given by $N_k = \sum \sigma_k[m]$.

\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

Starting from the pristine $H=0$ boundary state, the following macroscopic phenomena emerge consistently as entropy increases:

\textbf{Zero entropy yields a structureless state.} The all-zero configuration contains no detectable structural matches ($N_G = 0$), mapping naturally under any relational decoding scheme to a single, unextended spatial point.

\textbf{Spatial expansion.} As entropy grows, the density of $1$-bits climbs toward $0.5$, precipitating a rapid freeze-out of background $L_0$ tokens. The decoded spatial extent expands outward from the initial singularity.

\textbf{Lognormal abundance of structures.} Because higher-tier matter scales are governed by nested density constraints, their population curves ($N_1, N_2, N_3$) follow non-monotonic, rise-and-fall trajectories resembling lognormal distributions. These populations peak sequentially as a function of entropy before late-stage entropic degradation takes over.

As maximum entropy is approached ($H \to 1$), complex structural hierarchies dissolve. The bound elements break apart, reverting into unaligned radiation-like degrees of freedom—reproducing an informational analogue of cosmic heat death.

\subsection{The Informational Brake and Relational Scaling Geometry}

A critical result of this multi-scale framework is the emergence of a dynamic, self-stabilizing expansion geometry governed by structural token conservation logic. Because higher-level matter structures are built by grouping lower-level components, they effectively ``swallow'' or bound those sub-grid bits, withdrawing them from the free background metric.

To accurately deduce the true spatial geometry without artifact nesting or scale-trapping, we calculate the net un-swallowed, independent structural units remaining active at any instant. Subject to an observer visibility horizon controlled by maximum recursion depth $R \in \{0, 1, 2, 3\}$, the number of active relational elements $N_{active}$ is given by:
\[
N_{active} = \tilde{N}_0 + \sum_{k=1}^{R} \tilde{N}_k
\]
where the net, exclusive token counts $\tilde{N}_k$ are constrained by cascading down-sampling subtractions:
\begin{align*}
\tilde{N}_3 &= N_3 \\
\tilde{N}_2 &= \max(0, N_2 - N_3 \cdot w_4) \\
\tilde{N}_1 &= \max(0, N_1 - N_2 \cdot w_3) \\
\tilde{N}_0 &= \max(0, N_G - N_1 \cdot w_2)
\end{align*}

The instantaneous, relational spatial diameter of the horizon ($a_t$) is a direct function of these active elements relative to the total base resolution ceiling of the background fabric:
\[
a_t = \left( \frac{N_{active}}{N_{max}} \right) \cdot 2.0
\]

When mapped onto a symmetric coordinate distribution via alternating index parity signs ($\pm 1$), this metric formulation naturally dictates the spacetime profiles perceived by internal observers:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Inflationary Burst:} An initial, explosive expansion driven by the rapid, combinatorial generation of the primary $L_0$ space fabric as entropy departs from zero.
    \item \textbf{The Informational Brake:} An intermediate deceleration phase. As matter precipitates ($N_1, N_2$ grow), large blocks of background fabric are consumed by structures, putting a gentle, mathematical brake on the expansion velocity.
    \item \textbf{Late-Time Cosmic Drift:} As entropy drives the ultimate decay of higher-order structures back into the background vacuum, the localized matter constraints ease. The expansion rate asymptotes smoothly to a stable, positive Hubble coasting phase without requiring an explicit dark energy terms or a cosmological constant.
\end{itemize}

\section{Filter Independence}
\label{sec:robustness}

The most significant result of this paper is not the lognormal distribution
itself but its universality. We tested more than two hundred distinct filter
definitions, including:

\begin{itemize}
\item Geometric filters with varying distance thresholds $\delta_1,
      \delta_2, \delta_3$ spanning two orders of magnitude;
\item Geometric filters using different hierarchy definitions (pairs,
      triplets, quadruplets; equilateral, isoceles, and arbitrary
      configurations);
\item Different decoding maps $D$, including linear, logarithmic, and
      random projections from bitstrings to spatial coordinates;
\item Recursive pattern-matching filters with varying substring lengths $k$
      and varying composition rules;
\item Arbitrary pattern counting: selecting a fixed pattern at random ---
      for example, $010110100010$ --- and counting its occurrences in $S_t$
      as a function of entropy level.
\end{itemize}

In every case, the qualitative result is the same: zero structure at zero
entropy, a lognormal-shaped emergence curve, and long-tailed decay at high
entropy. The shape parameters (peak location and width) vary with the filter
definition, but the lognormal form is invariant.

The filter-independence extends across representational levels. Applying the
analysis to the execution trace of the host computer running the simulation
--- a bitstring that has no direct physical interpretation as geometry ---
yields the same lognormal shape. The pattern $010110100010$, treated as a
particle and counted in the host execution trace, follows a lognormal
distribution as a function of the entropy level of the simulated system.

This representational independence is the key result. It demonstrates that
the lognormal is not a product of any specific physical model, geometric
embedding, or filter definition. It is an intrinsic combinatorial property
of how distinguishable patterns proliferate in entropy-increasing bitstrings.
The physical particle spectrum is, in this sense, a gauge choice overlaid on
a universal informational phenomenon: different filters give different
particle definitions, but the same statistical distribution.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Expansion as entropy increase}

The identification of cosmic expansion with entropy increase is not merely
analogical. In the \iame{} framework, the geometric and informational
descriptions of a physical configuration are the same object viewed through
different lenses. The statement that the universe is expanding is, in this
framework, the geometric reading of the statement that the entropy of the
underlying configuration is increasing. These are not two correlated
phenomena but one phenomenon with two descriptions.

This has a sharp implication. The arrow of time, the thermodynamic second
law, and the expansion of the universe are not three independent features of
the observed world. They are one feature: the monotonic increase of
informational entropy, which reads as temporal succession from the
observer's internal perspective, as expansion from the geometric perspective,
and as entropy increase from the thermodynamic perspective.

\subsection{The Relational Metric: Matter Decay as the Origin of the Hubble Constant}
\label{subsec:relational_metric}

A critical challenge for informational cosmological models relying on raw entropy progression is the "freeze-out" problem: as the global bitstring approaches maximum thermodynamic equilibrium ($H \to 1$), the net rate of entropy change ($\Delta H / \Delta t$) asymptotically approaches zero, implying a stalling cosmic expansion rate. 

The \iame{} framework resolves this paradox natively by enforcing the principle of observer relational metrics. An embedded observer, structurally composed of Level 1 particles, can only measure spatial distances relative to the abundance of those foundational structures. The perceived geometric volume of the spatial manifold is fundamentally co-dependent on the total population sum of Level 1 tokens.

During the intermediate entropy eras, the creation of Level 2 Atoms and Level 3 Molecules locks multiple Level 1 constituents into localized, higher-order macro-tokens, effectively binding these degrees of freedom. However, as the system rushes toward maximum randomization, these higher-order structures inevitably decay. The dissolution of Level 3 and Level 2 clusters liberates their constituents, triggering a massive, late-stage surge in independent Level 1 particle counts.

Because the observer’s spatial metric is tied directly to this foundational population, the physical evaporation of matter is interpreted internally as a constant, driven expansion of space. Quantum "dark energy" and the observed Hubble constant ($H_0$) are thus stripped of their status as external parameters; they emerge natively as the geometric reading of macro-structural decay within a saturating information field.

\subsection{Resolution of the initial conditions problem}

The standard formulation of the initial conditions problem asks why the
universe began in such a low-entropy, highly ordered state. This question
presupposes that low-entropy initial states are improbable and require
explanation. Within the \iame{} framework, the zero-entropy initial state
requires no explanation at all: it is the unique, maximally compressible
configuration. Under the compression measure $P(c) \propto 2^{-\mathcal{C}(c)}$,
the zero-entropy state has the shortest description of any state in the
configuration space. It dominates the measure not by accident but because it
is the simplest possible starting point. The question is not why the universe
began in a low-entropy state but why one would expect anything else.

\subsection{Comparison with Penrose's Weyl curvature hypothesis}

Penrose's Weyl curvature hypothesis \cite{penrose1979} proposes that the
thermodynamic arrow of time originates in the asymmetry between initial and
final singularities: the initial singularity had vanishing Weyl curvature
(smooth, ordered), while final singularities (black holes) have diverging
Weyl curvature (chaotic, disordered). This is a geometric statement about
the initial state, motivated by the need to explain the arrow of time.

The present results go further in two respects. First, the initial state
here is not merely smooth within quantum limits but perfectly smooth:
zero entropy, uniquely determined, admitting no fluctuations. The universe
this generates is already rich enough to reproduce the qualitative features
of matter organisation and spatial expansion. No residual quantum
irregularities are required. Second, the smoothness of the initial state is
not an assumption but a derivation: within the \iame{} framework, the
compression measure selects the zero-entropy initial state as the most
probable starting point, so a smooth beginning is the expected outcome, not
a lucky accident.

\subsection{The Boltzmann brain problem}

A persistent difficulty for frameworks that postulate a large or infinite
configuration space is the Boltzmann brain problem: in a sufficiently large
ensemble, random high-entropy fluctuations that momentarily produce a
conscious observer are exponentially more numerous than structured,
persistent observers embedded in a low-entropy universe. The compression
measure of the \iame{} framework suppresses these fluctuations directly:
high-entropy configurations have high description length and are
exponentially disfavoured. The lognormal emergence profile is the
quantitative signature of this suppression: structure peaks at intermediate
entropy and decays at high entropy, so the highest-entropy configurations
are precisely the ones with the least structure, not the most.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper establishes three foundational results within the \iame{} framework.

First, cosmic expansion is shown to be the pure geometric interpretation of increasing informational entropy.
Beginning from the unique zero-entropy state identified in Paper~II as the informational singularity, entropy-driven mutations drive the systematic expansion of the spatial manifold without requiring a cosmological constant, ad-hoc inflaton fields, or hard-coded equations of motion.

Second, a realistic particle abundance spectrum emerges natively from a multi-scale, recursive observer downsampling filter.
By shifting from rigid pattern-matching to convolutional density horizons governed by independent window sizes $W_k$ and activation thresholds $T_k$, the model breaks combinatorial parallel symmetry.
This yields a healthy population cascade: an early saturation of Level 1 Hadrons, followed by the localized crystallization of Level 2 Atoms, and a distinct, delayed evolutionary era for Level 3 Compound Molecules.

Third, by enforcing a background-independent, relational spatial metric anchored to the maximum extent of the observer's active Level 1 horizon, the simulation resolves the thermodynamic freeze-out paradox.
As the global information field approaches maximum equilibrium and macro-matter decays, the sudden late-stage liberation of constituent Level 1 particles warps the spatial coordinates.
The universe undergoes a non-linear geometric transition from a flat Euclidean section into a hyperbolic, Lorentzian spacetime cone. 

The observed Hubble constant ($H_0$) and the illusion of an accelerating cosmic expansion rate are thus stripped of their status as external parameters; they emerge natively as the internal geometric reading of structural evaporation within a saturating informational ontology.
